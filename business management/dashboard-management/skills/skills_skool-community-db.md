## Post Structures

### Inspirational Structure

```
TITLE
Short, direct — a statement or incomplete thought that pulls the reader in.
E.g. "Don't Give Up...Yet"

OLLY'S MOMENT
One specific moment from his own experience. Brief context — what was happening?

THE PAIN (mirroring)
What chronic illness does beyond the physical — the numbness, the loss of joy,
the disconnection. This is where members feel seen.

THE TURN
What started to change. Heart Coherence, consistent practice, finding the light again.

HONEST CAVEAT
It takes time and consistent practice. No false promises. This builds trust.

CLOSE
A personal commitment to be there for them. Warm, not performative.
E.g. "I will be here for you as you do that."
```

### Engaging Structure

```
TITLE
Simple and direct. E.g. "How are you?"

OPENER
"Hey..." — casual, no preamble.

THE QUESTION(S)
1 to 3 short questions. What are they working on? What do they need? How are they feeling?
For polls: 4 options, simple language, relatable to where they actually are.

No sign-off needed — the question is the close.
```

### Technical Structure

```
TITLE
➡️ What To Do When [the difficulty]
The arrow emoji in the title signals practical help — use it consistently.

OPEN WITH THE EXPERIENCE
"If you've tried... / If you've ever noticed..." — broad, general, never "someone on the call."

NORMALISE
"That's absolutely normal." Say it directly.

EXPLAIN WHY (simply)
Body in protective mode, armour, survival state — plain language, no jargon.

THE PATH FORWARD
What consistent Heart Coherence practice does to address this. Gradual, honest.

THE RESULT
What it feels like when the body starts to let go. Specific and grounded.

CLOSE
"Any questions?" — opens the door without pressure.
```

## Format Rules

- **Line length:** One or two sentences per line maximum. Never long paragraphs.
- **White space:** Heavy. Blank line between every line. Skool reads like a chat feed.
- **Rhythm:** Ellipsis (...) used for slow, reading-pace rhythm — particularly in Inspirational and Technical posts. Natural, not mechanical.
- **Emoji:** 0 to 1 per post. Technical posts use ➡️ in the title only.
- **No em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators.** Use a full stop instead.
- **Spelling:** British English throughout — no exceptions.
- **Sign-off options:**
  - Warm close: `Olly` or `To your health, happiness and success. Olly`
  - Engagement close: end with a question — no sign-off needed
  - Technical close: `Any questions?`
- **Banned words:** game changer, deep dive, synergy, leverage (verb), hustle, grind, low-hanging fruit, unlock, skyrocket, supercharge

---

## Tone Rules

- Write as Olly — first person, honest, warm, never preachy
- Peer framing, not expert-to-patient — "I know the darkness well", not coach-to-student
- Meet members inside their experience before moving to the solution
- Never promise a cure or overnight results — gradual, consistent, real
- Plain language: "survival mode", "body feels safe", "find the light again", "heart comes back online"
- Never clinical, never wellness-influencer, never a sales pitch
- The community already knows the story — no need to over-explain Heart Coherence on every post

## Learning From Posts Marked "Made Changes"

Olly uses the **Made Changes** status to flag posts that were generated correctly but needed editing before publishing. These are not failures — the post was usable — but the edits reveal something worth improving.

**When to check:** Before generating any new batch of posts, always check for posts marked "Made Changes" first.

**What to do:**

1. Pull all posts with Status = "Made Changes" from the database
2. For each one, read the current post copy (Olly's edited version) carefully
3. Try to identify what was likely changed — tone, phrasing, structure, specific words, length, rhythm
4. Note any patterns across multiple "Made Changes" posts — these reveal a systematic gap in the generation rules
5. Apply those learnings immediately when writing the new batch
6. If the pattern is clear and recurring, update the relevant section of this skills file so it is baked in permanently

**What to look for:**
- Words or phrases Olly consistently removes or rewrites — add them to the Banned Words list or Tone Rules
- Structural changes — if Olly regularly shortens a section, tighten that section in future
- Tone adjustments — if posts are made warmer, more direct, less preachy, update the Tone Rules
- Rhythm changes — if ellipsis or line breaks are adjusted, note the pattern

**Important:** Never ask Olly to explain what he changed. Read the edited copy and work it out. The goal is that "Made Changes" posts become less frequent over time as the generation rules improve.
