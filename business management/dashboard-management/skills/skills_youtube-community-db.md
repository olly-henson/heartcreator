## Personal Story Structure

```
HOOK
One line that pulls the reader in. Bold statement, relatable question, or
"I didn't think it was possible..." style opener.

SETUP
Brief context — what was life like before? 2-4 lines max.

SYMPTOMS / WHAT IT LOOKED LIKE
List using ⏩ arrows. 4-8 items. Lead with the specific symptom the audience
will recognise — exhaustion, pain, brain fog, emotional reactivity.
Never lead with "chronic illness" as the hook — lead with the symptom.

TURNING POINT
What changed. "Things changed when..." / "The day things turned around..."

THE MECHANISM
Heart Coherence — introduce naturally, not as a sales pitch.
Always: "something called Heart Coherence" — never assume they know what it is.

THE RESULT
What life looks like now. Specific and grounded, not vague wellness promises.

CLOSE
CTA, soft sign-off, or engagement question.
```

## Belief-Changing Post Structure

The goal is to take the audience from "I'm not sure healing is possible for me" to "there is something I haven't tried properly, and it might be the missing piece." One belief per post. Pick from the list below or use real language from `prospect_intel.md`.

**Beliefs worth targeting (drawn from real prospect language):**
- "I can't make it work for me" (Isabell)
- "I've tried everything and nothing lasts"
- "I have a lot of knowledge about this but it wasn't enough" (cancer survivor prospect)
- "I feel I'll never be normal again" (Ana D)
- "Doctors put so much fear in me"
- "I just don't have the energy to try another thing"
- "I know healing is possible — just not for me"
- "Every treatment works for a bit, then I go back to square one"

```
HOOK — THE BELIEF
State the belief directly. Use the audience's exact words where possible.
E.g. "I can't make it work for me."
Or: "I know healing is possible. I just don't think it's possible for me."

VALIDATE
This belief makes complete sense given what they've been through.
Don't dismiss it — meet it with honesty.
"If you've spent years trying things that haven't lasted, of course you'd feel that way."

REFRAME
The belief is a logical response to the wrong mechanism — not evidence that healing isn't possible.
The treatments didn't work because they don't address the root cause.
The body is still stuck in fight or flight. That's the loop. That's what nothing has broken yet.

THE SHIFT
Introduce Heart Coherence as the missing piece — gently, not as a pitch.
Always: "something called Heart Coherence" — never assume they know what it is.
One or two lines only. The point is the belief shift, not the mechanism explanation.

CLOSE
Soft and warm. No CTA required — but can be used selectively (roughly every other post).
Options:
- A reflective line that lands the belief shift ("It wasn't you. It was the mechanism.")
- Standard CTA if appropriate
- Or simply: "To your health, happiness and success. Olly"
```

**What makes a strong Belief-Changing post:**
- It names the belief in the audience's own words — not a paraphrase
- It never makes the audience feel foolish for holding the belief
- The reframe is logical, not motivational — it should feel like an explanation, not a pep talk
- It's short. These are not long-form essays. 150–250 words is the sweet spot.
- The belief shift lands in the final line before the close — that's the punchline

## Format Rules

- **Line length:** One or two sentences per line maximum. Never long paragraphs.
- **White space:** Heavy. **Every single line or phrase must be separated by a blank line** — not just grouped paragraphs. This is critical: when posts are written without a blank line between every line, the spacing collapses when copied from Notion, making posts read as dense blocks. Even a one-liner like "But that didn't stop me..." gets its own blank line above and below.
- **Ellipsis:** Use ... for pacing and dramatic rhythm in personal story posts. "Back in 2018..." / "But that didn't stop me..."
- **Emoji:** Use where they fit naturally — not as a rule. A single relevant emoji can work at the top of a post if it adds to the feel (e.g. 💚 for a healing story). ⏩ for lists. Never forced. Never mid-post unless it genuinely fits.
- **No hyphens as clause separators.** Use a full stop instead.
- **Sign-off:** "Olly" — no emoji as a rule. Occasionally "Olly 😎" if it fits naturally.
- **Spelling:** British English throughout. Casual and real — "I didn't half arse it either" is fine.
- **Arrow style:** ⏩ — consistent throughout.

## CTAs

**Standard CTA (use selectively — not on every Personal Story):**
```
If you'd like to learn how to regulate your nervous system with Heart Coherence so that you can get your life back, check out the link to my free community in the comments below.
```

> **Important:** Do not use the standard CTA at the end of every Personal Story post. Overusing it makes the feed feel repetitive and salesy. Aim for roughly every other personal story. When skipping the CTA, use a soft close or engagement question instead.

**Soft close (for Personal Story posts without a CTA, or Personal Update posts):**
```
To your health, happiness and success.
Olly
```

Or simply a warm, reflective final line that fits the story — e.g. "I hope you do too. Olly" or "Great memories! 😎"

**Engagement close (for Engagement posts):**
End with the question. No explicit CTA needed — the comments are the mechanism.

- Honest and casual — "I didn't half arse it either"
- No hyphens as clause separators
- No corporate wellness language
