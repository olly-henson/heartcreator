## Email A — Personal Story (curiosity subject line)

**Subject:** The dog wouldn't stop pacing

Hi {{contact.first_name}},

A few years back I had a dog who used to do this thing before it rained.

She'd pace.

Sniff around.

Get into position.

Then change her mind and start all over again.

It used to wind me up no end. Just go, I'd think.

But the more I watched her, the more I realised how familiar it looked.

Because that's exactly what I used to do with my own life.

Get into position to change something.

Start the meditation. Say the affirmation. Picture the future.

Then talk myself out of it halfway through.

Not because I didn't want it.

Because some part of me didn't feel safe enough to commit to it.

That's not a discipline problem.

It's a nervous system problem.

When your body doesn't feel safe, it will always find a reason to pace instead of go.

That's the part most people trying to consciously create their life are never told.

You can want the change with everything in you.

But if your nervous system is still bracing for impact, you'll circle it instead of stepping into it.

Something called Heart Coherence is what finally got my body to stop pacing and just go.

Olly

## Email B — Belief-Changing (contrast subject line)

**Subject:** Feel less. Create more.

Hi {{contact.first_name}},

Most people trying to consciously create a new life think the answer is to feel their future harder.

Visualise more vividly.

Say the affirmation with more conviction.

Journal about it until it feels real.

I get why. It's what everyone teaches.

But what if the problem was never how hard you were trying to feel it?

What if it's that you're trying to feel it from a body that's still bracing for a threat?

Here's the thing nobody tells you.

Your subconscious mind only responds to feeling.

But if your nervous system is stuck in survival, the feelings it produces are doubt, urgency and effort. Not the calm, embodied belief that actually gets remembered.

So you push harder.

Still nothing sticks.

You go and learn another technique, hoping that one will be different.

It can go on for years.

It's just because your body is still mainly operating from a survival based state, not because you're doing anything wrong.

Once you get out of your head and into your heart, this changes.

Olly

## Email C — Q&A (straight benefit subject line)

**Subject:** Why the feeling never lasts

Someone messaged me this recently and I think a lot of people reading this will relate.

"I can feel it sometimes. In meditation, or right after I journal. But by the next day it's gone and I'm back to doubting everything."

I hear a version of this most weeks.

Here's what's actually happening.

That flicker of feeling is real. It's not imagined and it's not you being inconsistent.

The problem is where it's coming from.

If it shows up during a practice and disappears the moment life resumes, it means your body hasn't actually shifted state. You borrowed the feeling for a few minutes. You didn't change the environment it has to survive in for the rest of the day.

Your nervous system will always default back to whatever state it spends the most time in.

So if survival mode is still the default setting, the calm, confident feeling from your morning practice was never going to hold past breakfast.

This is why regulating the nervous system has to come before anything else. Not another technique. The actual foundation underneath all of them.

Olly

## Email D — Checklist (numbers subject line)

**Subject:** 3 things keeping the old you around

Hi {{contact.first_name}},

If you've been trying to consciously change your life and it keeps feeling like the old version of you is still running the show, it's usually one of these three things.

1. You're trying to think your way into a new identity, when your subconscious only responds to feeling.

2. Your nervous system is still operating from survival most of the day, which blocks access to the elevated emotions your future self actually feels like.

3. You're expecting one big breakthrough moment, when it's actually built through small, repeated, felt experiences of already being her.

None of these mean you're doing it wrong.

They just mean the missing piece was never another technique.

It was getting your body into a state where feeling your future is actually possible.

Olly
