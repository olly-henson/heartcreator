# Prospect Avatars — Olly Henson Coaching

> Real prospect profiles. Use these when writing Reel headlines, captions, emails, and any content where a specific person needs to be held in mind. Content must speak to *these* people — not a generic personal development audience.

## Primary avatar — Rachel

**Always write to Rachel first.** She is the creative anchor for all content. Full profile in `avatar-rachel.md`.

Rachel is a seeker. She has invested deeply in manifestation, reality creation and conscious creation. She understands the concepts intellectually. She cannot make them work in practice. The primary reason: survival mode keeps her in her analytical mind (high beta brainwaves), which blocks access to her subconscious and makes it impossible to genuinely feel her future. She wants a relationship, time and financial freedom, and to live from her passions rather than her corporate career.

**The niche is reality creation, manifestation and subconscious reprogramming.** The primary audience is people trying to create a new reality — health, wealth, relationships, or identity — who are stuck at the feeling/implementation gap. The manifestation audience (Abraham Hicks, Neville Goddard, Dispenza followers) is the primary crossover. Chronic illness is a valid subset but is not the lead positioning.

## Consciousness Level

The ideal 1-2-1 client sits between Courage (200) and Love (500) on David Hawkins' Map of Consciousness. Most typically in the Neutrality to Reason band (250–400).

| Level | Log | Emotional State | View of Life |
|-------|-----|----------------|--------------|
| Reason | 400 | Understanding | Meaningful |
| Acceptance | 350 | Forgiveness | Harmonious |
| Willingness | 310 | Optimism | Hopeful |
| Neutrality | 250 | Trust | Satisfactory |
| Courage | 200 | Affirmation | Feasible |

Content speaks across this full band — not just the middle. Touch on Courage to meet those who are ready to act. Touch on Love to call forward those who sense something larger is possible. The primary register is Neutrality through Reason.

**The emotional tone is frustration at the mechanics — not at themselves.** Why isn't this working? Why does it keep resetting? What am I missing? That is the dominant feeling. Not self-blame. Not despair.

Personal pain — the recognition that significant time has passed without the expected change — is used sparingly and deliberately. It lands hard precisely because it isn't used constantly.

**In practice this means:**
- Content is emotional but not pain-focused
- Lead with emotional recognition (brief and real), follow with intellectual explanation
- The reader should feel recognised as capable and curious — not rescued
- They have the stability to invest time, energy and money
- They can implement consistently once given the right foundation

Content that speaks primarily to despair, apathy or crisis (below Courage) will attract people who are not yet in a position to implement or invest — avoid.

## What All Five Have in Common

These shared patterns apply across every avatar. Content that names this shared experience will stop them cold.

### The Shared Journey

Every avatar has been through the same progression:

1. **Something isn't working** — health, wealth, relationships, or simply feeling like themselves
2. **They tried the conventional route** — doctors, therapists, traditional approaches — with limited results
3. **They discovered personal development / mind-body healing** — Dispenza, Lipton, Moorjani, Neville Goddard, Abraham Hicks — it resonated deeply
4. **They went all in** — read the books, tried the techniques, invested time and money
5. **They got some results but they didn't last** — the inspired feeling doesn't stick, the crash always comes
6. **They are stuck at implementation** — can't bridge theory into daily lived experience alone. Can understand it. Can't feel it.

They are not sceptics. They are **believers stuck at the door.**

### The Implementation Gap

The defining pain of this audience. Not an information problem. Not an understanding problem. Not a consistency problem.

The problem is that they are trying to create from the wrong place. From the head. From fight/flight. And you cannot reprogram the subconscious from the head.

*"Another week. And nothing's changed."* — this is where they live.

### The Restart Cycle

New inspiration. New technique. New programme. Feel like it's working for a few days. Crash back to the same state. Repeat. They are trapped in a loop that no amount of information can break — because the loop is being driven by the nervous system, not by a lack of knowledge.

### The Identity Erosion

They have quietly begun to wonder if they are the problem. The person who was once excited about transformation is now quietly losing faith. This layer is often more painful than the original problem — and rarely spoken about.

### The Unasked Questions They Carry Silently

These are the things they wonder but never say out loud. Content that voices these will stop them immediately:

- "Why does everyone else seem to be able to feel this and I can't?"
- "Why do I understand it completely but feel nothing when I try?"
- "What is wrong with me?"
- "Am I just not capable of making this work?"
- "Is the life I'm trying to create actually possible for someone like me?"
- "How much longer can I keep trying before I give up?"

### What They Actually Want

Not transformation as a concept. Specific, ordinary moments they can picture:

- Waking up and genuinely feeling like the person they've been working to become
- Making a plan and keeping it
- Feeling an elevated emotion that doesn't disappear after five minutes
- Looking at their life and recognising it as the one they were creating
- Trusting themselves

**When writing content, name one of these specific moments rather than the abstract concept. That specificity is what makes them feel seen.**

### What They're Wary Of

They have been disappointed many times. They are cautious of:
- Another programme that becomes just more information
- Overpromised results that don't last
- Being sold to before being understood
- Feeling manipulated or patronised

**Content that acknowledges this directly — rather than ignoring it — builds far more trust than content that simply makes promises.**
