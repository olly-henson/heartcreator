## Exercise Packaging

**Pattern: Specific, simple, numbered**

Chris's exercise: tally counter or prayer beads, 50 reps, 5 times a day, 10 days.

What makes it work:
- Rachel is exhausted by complexity — she's done hour-long meditations, retreats, courses
- A numbered, timed, repeatable exercise feels achievable
- The specificity ("10 days") makes it feel like a real commitment with a real end point
- She can start today with almost no setup

**Apply to Olly's Technique videos:**
- Where possible, give the practice a specific number or time — "5 minutes", "once a day for 7 days", "3 breaths"
- Frame the simplicity honestly — Heart Coherence is not complex. The packaging should reflect that.
- Avoid making it sound like another programme or protocol. It should feel like something she can do right now.

## Title Structures

**Pattern 1 — Personal story + tangible action (talking head):**
"I created a new reality by counting to 50"
→ First person, specific action, curiosity gap. Rachel wants what he had.

**Pattern 2 — Descriptive + outcome subtitle + parenthetical clarifier (meditation):**
"Fall Asleep Manifestation Meditation: Shift timelines in days (Create new memories)"
→ Main title says what it is. Subtitle says what it does. Parenthetical adds a second hook.

**Pattern 3 — Bold declarative claim addressing the objection directly:**
"This video will WITHOUT A DOUBT shift your reality."
→ No curiosity gap. Just a confident promise. "WITHOUT A DOUBT" in caps names Rachel's core block — her doubt — and dismisses it. Works because Rachel is not a sceptic. She wants to believe. Give her permission to.

**Pattern 4 — Personal + parenthetical sincerity flag:**
"This manifestation video is from YOURSELF (I mean it)"
→ The parenthetical "(I mean it)" preempts scepticism — signals sincerity, not clickbait. "YOURSELF" in caps makes it feel personally addressed. Works when the video has an unusual or bold premise that Rachel might dismiss.

**Apply to Olly's titles:**
- Four different formats — not locked to one formula. Match the format to what the video is doing.
- Pattern 1 for Personal and Technique videos — Olly did something specific, Rachel wants the same result
- Pattern 2 for guided meditation content — clear, descriptive, outcome-led
- Pattern 3 for Argument-Based videos where the whole point is removing Rachel's doubt or reframing her belief
- Pattern 4 when the video has a premise that sounds too good or unusual — the parenthetical sincerity flag earns the click
- Parenthetical clarifiers and sincerity flags work throughout: "(I mean it)", "(this actually works)", "(not what you think)"

## Thumbnail Text Style and Layout

Chris uses three distinct thumbnail text approaches across his videos:

| Video | Text | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| Counting to 50 | "Too easy" | Pattern interrupt — she's been making it hard |
| Sleep meditation | "YOU CHOOSE PARADISE" | Empowering — she is the agent |
| Reality shift | "THIS IS IT" + "NEW REALITY WHILE WATCHING" | Stop-scrolling + removes action barrier |
| Video from yourself | "YOU HAVE IT ALL" | Declaration of what she already has — not a promise, a statement of truth |

**Two-text thumbnail layout (third video):**
- Large bold text top-left: the stop-scroll statement ("THIS IS IT")
- Smaller text bottom: the mechanism or promise ("NEW REALITY WHILE WATCHING")
- Arrow pointing from text toward face — directs the eye and positions Olly as the delivery mechanism
- Creates a visual hierarchy: what it is → what it does → who delivers it

**"New reality while watching" device:**
The promise that the transformation happens during the video — not after she does anything — lowers the action barrier dramatically. She doesn't need to implement. She just needs to watch. Apply this to Technique and Mission-Based videos where the video itself contains a live practice.

**The master rule across all four thumbnails:**
Every piece of text speaks TO Rachel about HER — never about Olly, never about the content. Her ease, her power, her arrival, her completeness. The thumbnail text is always addressed to the viewer.

**Face expression variety:**
- Knowing smile looking at camera (confident, "I know something you don't")
- Emotional moment, hands pressed, eyes closed (genuine feeling, "what is he experiencing?")
Both work. Different curiosity hooks. Use the emotional expression for videos where the felt experience IS the point.

**Split thumbnail layout:**
AI image left + face right works when the AI image needs to carry meaning of its own (the time machine portal, the cosmic head). Label the AI image when it needs naming — "TIMEMACHINE" with an arrow. Don't leave it ambiguous.

**Scientific diagram as thumbnail image (Braden clip channel):**
The HeartMath HRV graphs (jagged frustration line vs smooth coherence wave) work as a thumbnail image for Educational content. Rachel sees the contrast at a glance — no reading required. The diagram is the argument. For Olly's Educational videos about heart coherence specifically, the coherence vs incoherence graph is a ready-made thumbnail visual that communicates the core science immediately.

| Thumbnail image type | Best used for |
|---|---|
| Physical tools (prayer beads, tally counter) | Technique videos with a tangible practice |
| AI cosmic imagery (portal, galaxy head) | Mission-Based, meditation, big-picture content |
| Scientific diagram (HRV graph, brain-heart diagram) | Educational content — science as the hook |
| Olly's face alone (various expressions) | Personal, Argument-Based |

**Technical specificity in thumbnail text:**
"3-MINUTE SECRET / 0.1HZ FREQUENCY" — precision signals credibility. A specific number or measurement in thumbnail text (not just "easy" or "simple") works for science-led Educational content. Apply sparingly — only when the specificity IS the hook.

**Apply to Olly's thumbnails:**
- Thumbnail text always speaks to Rachel about Rachel — never describes the video content
- Two-text layouts: stop-scroll statement (top/large) + mechanism or promise (bottom/smaller)
- Match the left-side image type to the content type (see table above)
- Emotional face expression valid for Technique and Mission videos
- Scientific diagrams valid for Educational content — the HeartMath coherence graph is ready to use
- Label AI images when they carry meaning — don't leave the metaphor unexplained
- Olly's existing cosmic/space brand aesthetic matches Chris's meditation thumbnail almost exactly — validated
- Short, all caps; magenta accent on one word for emphasis

- Deliver the lesson (he wasn't asking for rain — he was feeling it as already done)

Short, real, specific, multi-sensory, surprising. Olly has his own demonstration stories (the $13k month, the chronic illness recovery). Structure them the same way — specific sensory detail, unexpected action, undeniable result, clean lesson.
