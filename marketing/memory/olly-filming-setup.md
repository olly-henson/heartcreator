# Olly Filming Setup

> Reference document for Olly and Claude. Contains everything needed to recreate the filming setup consistently for every YouTube shoot.

---

## The Space

- **Filming position:** Olly sits at his desk in the home office/alcove, facing OUT toward the lounge
- **Background:** The lounge — grey curtains, sofa, TV wall
- **Skylight:** Directly above Olly's head at the desk. Must film after dark or fit a blackout blind

---

| Warm desk lamp (white gooseneck) | Key light | Right side of desk, angled at face |

## Lighting Notes

### Key Light — warm desk lamp (Olly's right)
- The dominant light source. Warm/incandescent bulb.
- Angled toward Olly's face from the right side.
- Creates shadow on the left side of his face — this is the depth and mood.
- During the day, the skylight overpowers it. Must film at night.

### Fill Light — Horizum ring (Olly's left)
- Softer, cooler light from the opposite side.
- Keeps the shadow side of the face from going completely dark.
- Should be noticeably dimmer than the key light to preserve depth.

### Background Lamps — floor lamp + extra lamp (in lounge)
- Both ON when filming.
- Creates warm, ambient glow in the background — similar to the vertical light accent Chris The Wiz Miller uses.
- Floor lamp on Olly's right, extra lamp on Olly's left.

### What to turn OFF
- Overhead spotlights in the office — flatten the face, kill all depth
- Lounge overhead lights — background should be darker than Olly's face
- TV — black screen is cleaner than a reflective grey one

## Background Notes

- **Grey curtains** across the back wall are the primary backdrop — close them every shoot
- **Sofa** visible on left — keep it tidy, remove throws or clutter before filming
- **Patterned chair** on right — move out of frame every shoot
- **Coffee table** — clear the remote and anything else off it
- **TV** — turn off

---

## Style Reference — Chris The Wiz Miller

- Dark, moody look — face well-lit, background significantly darker
- Strong single key light creating shadow on one side of face
- Background has a subtle warm accent light (not dark void, but not bright either)
- Close to camera — medium shot, chest up
- Direct eye contact with lens
- Casual clothing, dark tones work well

---

## Pre-Shoot Checklist

- [ ] Film after dark (skylight off)
- [ ] Close lounge curtains
- [ ] Turn off office overhead spotlights
- [ ] Turn off lounge overhead lights
- [ ] TV off
- [ ] Coffee table cleared
- [ ] Patterned chair moved out of frame
- [ ] iPhone on tripod — right side of desk, right of laptop, left of key light
- [ ] Key light (warm desk lamp) on — angled at face from right
- [ ] Horizum ring on — left side, dimmer than key
- [ ] Both background lamps on (floor lamp + extra lamp in lounge)
- [ ] Sit down and check framing before recording

## Future Upgrade (optional)

If daytime filming becomes important, options are:
1. **Skylight blackout blind** — Velux or generic Amazon blind, ~£30–80, one-off fix
2. **Stronger key light** — LED panel (Elgato Key Light or Godox equivalent) powerful enough to overpower the skylight
