# YouTube Thumbnail Shot List
> One session. Landscape (horizontal iPhone). Dark room, lights set up per filming setup doc.
> Chest-up frame. Leave room either side for text overlays.

---

## Before You Start
- Set up exactly as per the filming setup doc (evening, lights on, curtains closed)
- iPhone in landscape on tripod
- Wear 2–3 different tops (dark tones work best — navy, charcoal, olive, black)
- Have someone trigger the camera remotely, OR use the self-timer (3–10 seconds)
- Take 5–10 shots per pose — you need options

---

## The Shot List

### 1. The Direct — serious, locked in
**Expression:** Straight face, eyes directly into lens. No smile. Slight forward lean.
**Body:** Shoulders square to camera. Chin very slightly down.
**Think:** "I know something you need to hear."
**Works for:** "Why your manifestation isn't working", "The truth about..."

---

### 2. The Revelation — wide eyes, something just clicked
**Expression:** Eyes wide, eyebrows raised, mouth slightly open as if mid-realisation.
**Body:** Slight lean back, one hand to chest or chin.
**Think:** The moment something you believed turned out to be completely wrong.
**Works for:** "I finally understood why...", "Nobody tells you this..."

---

### 3. The Point — direct, assertive
**Expression:** Serious or slightly intense. Eyes locked on lens.
**Body:** One index finger pointing directly at the camera/viewer.
**Think:** "This is about YOU specifically."
**Works for:** "You need to hear this", "This is why YOU can't..."

---

### 4. The Knowing Smile — confident, warm
**Expression:** Slight smile, not a full grin. One corner of the mouth. Eyes engaged.
**Body:** Relaxed. Maybe arms crossed loosely or one hand resting.
**Think:** You've been where they are and you know exactly how this ends.
**Works for:** "Once you get this, everything changes", lighter/aspirational titles

---

### 5. The Lean In — conspiratorial, "let me tell you something"
**Expression:** Slightly narrowed eyes, small intense smile. Almost a whisper energy.
**Body:** Upper body leaning toward the camera. Hands optionally clasped or one gesturing.
**Think:** You're about to share a secret with a close friend.
**Works for:** "Here's what they don't tell you about...", "The real reason..."

---

### 6. The Disbelief — raised eyebrow, questioning
**Expression:** One eyebrow raised significantly higher than the other. Slight smirk or neutral mouth.
**Body:** Head tilted very slightly to one side.
**Think:** Someone just said something you can't believe people still believe.
**Works for:** "Still doing this?", "You think THIS is the problem..."

---

### 7. The Open Hands — honest, nothing to hide
**Expression:** Calm, genuine, warm. Slight smile optional.
**Body:** Both hands open and facing camera, raised to chest/shoulder height. Like "here's everything I've got."
**Think:** Complete transparency — this is real, this is true.
**Works for:** Personal story content, "I'm going to be honest with you..."

---

### 8. The Look Away — thoughtful, contemplative
**Expression:** Neutral or slightly serious. Eyes looking off to one side (not at camera).
**Body:** Slight profile, chin up slightly.
**Think:** You're weighing something important. The quiet before the breakthrough.
**Works for:** Paired with a bold text overlay. Gives designer space to add context.

---

### 9. The Arms Crossed — authority, conviction
**Expression:** Calm, confident, direct eye contact.
**Body:** Arms folded across chest. Not aggressive — settled and sure.
**Think:** You've done the work. You're not guessing.
**Works for:** "Here's what actually works", confident/authority positioning titles

---

### 10. The Hands Up — surrender or emphasis
**Expression:** Eyebrows up, eyes wide, slight smile. Playful or emphatic.
**Body:** Both hands raised at shoulder height, palms out. Like "I'm telling you!"
**Think:** This is so obvious once you see it. Or: I can't believe this is real.
**Works for:** "It's this simple", "Too easy", lighter engagement titles (similar to Chris's "Too easy" thumbnail)

---

## Bonus Shots — Props

### With a book or notebook
Hold open a book/notebook toward camera. Serious expression. Works for study/research framing.

### Pointing to the side (at text)
Point to your left or right — leaves space for text overlay. Useful for graphic design.

### Eyes closed — meditative
Chin slightly up, eyes closed, peaceful expression. Works specifically for Heart Coherence / meditation content.

---

## Clothing Rotation
Shoot at least 3 tops so you have variety across future thumbnails without re-shooting.
- Dark top (navy/black/charcoal) — most versatile for dark background
- Mid tone (olive, grey, denim) — adds variety
- White or light — high contrast option, use sparingly
