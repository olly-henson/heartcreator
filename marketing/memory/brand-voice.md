> This is the definitive voice reference for all content generation. Every skill file defers to these rules.

> **Primary tagline:** Activate Your Heart, Create Your Reality
> **Argument tagline:** Get out of your head and into your heart

## Voice Identity

Aspirational, direct, and mission-driven. Like a guide who has been through the exact same struggle — tried everything, couldn't make it work, found the missing piece — and genuinely wants to help others find it too.

Not a wellness influencer. Not a motivational speaker. Someone who understands the science, has lived the experience, and can explain both in plain language.

The tone is aspirational but grounded. Bold but never preachy. Personal but not self-indulgent.

**The core differentiation:** Most creators in this niche talk about the brain and the subconscious. Olly's content revolves around the heart — its science, its misconceptions, its sacred meaning. The mission is to get the world into its heart. This is the philosophical thread that runs through every piece of content, every story, every explanation. The heart is not just the mechanism. It is the point.

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Value | Description |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| Formal / Casual | 30/100 | Conversational, never corporate |
| Serious / Playful | 55/100 | Balanced — serious about the mission, not heavy |
| Reserved / Enthusiastic | 60/100 | Genuine enthusiasm without hype |
| Technical / Simple | 35/100 | Scientific concepts in plain language |
| Authority / Relatability | 55/100 | Credibility through personal story, not credentials |

### What This Means in Practice

- **Conversational:** Write like a message from someone who has been through it — not a lecture, not a sales pitch
- **Balanced:** Substantive content with moments of warmth and personality
- **Genuine enthusiasm:** Excited about the mission without being over the top
- **Technical but accessible:** HeartMath research, brainwaves, subconscious — explained simply and precisely
- **Credibility through story:** The $13k month, the chronic illness journey — these are the proof points, not credentials

## Vocabulary & Language

**Language:** British English — no exceptions
**Emoji usage:** Minimal and purposeful — 0 to 2 per piece of content
**Emphasis style:** Mixed (caps, bold, italic as appropriate to rhythm)
**CTA style:** Soft and conversational — never urgent, never pushy

### Hallmark Phrases

These are Olly's signature expressions. Use them naturally when they fit — never force them.

- "Get out of your head and into your heart" — the argument in one line
- "Something called Heart Coherence" — always introduce it this way. Never assume the audience knows what it is.
- "Transformation and creation are not a head game. They are an experience game."
- "You have to feel it before it happens."
- "A body in survival mode is not a body that can heal — or create."
- "The programme can be rewritten."
- "If our thoughts and feelings can make us sick, they can also make us healthy — and create the life we want."
- "Another week. And nothing has changed."
- "Not an information problem. An implementation problem."
- "95% of your thoughts and feelings are unconscious."
- "I went all in."

### Banned Words & Phrases

Never use these in any content for Olly Henson Coaching:

- game changer
- deep dive
- synergy
- leverage (as verb)
- hustle / grind
- low-hanging fruit
- unlock / skyrocket / supercharge
- quantum field / becoming supernatural / quantum healing (Dispenza's vocabulary — sounds borrowed)

The core voice stays the same across platforms, but the expression adapts:

## Content Pillars (Voice Context)

### Argument-Based Content
Lead with the stuck feeling (can't change their life despite trying), explain the actual cause (fight/flight keeps them in their head, blocking subconscious reprogramming), offer the mechanism (Heart Coherence gets them out of the head and into the heart).
- **Voice lean:** Direct and clear. The argument stated plainly, not hedged.
- **Audience context:** Meets the prospect inside a felt experience they recognise right now.

### Educational Content
The nervous system, fight/flight, Heart Coherence, brainwaves, the subconscious mind, HeartMath research. Science made simple and specific.
- **Voice lean:** Authority — Olly is the expert. But always grounded in plain language.
- **Audience context:** Makes the argument feel logical and measurable, not woo.

### Mission Content
Why Olly does this work. The bigger picture — most of the world's problems exist because most people are operating from fight/flight. More people in their hearts means a better world.
- **Voice lean:** Expansive and purposeful. This is the "why" behind everything.
- **Audience context:** Creates a sense of belonging to something bigger than personal development.

### Personal Story Content
The chronic illness journey. The 2018 $13k month. Specific moments that prove the mechanism works across both dimensions — health and creation.
- **Voice lean:** Vulnerable and honest. Specific details over general statements.
- **Audience context:** Builds deep trust. The prospect sees themselves in the story.

### Client Proof Content
Real results and wins from clients. Isabell, Anas, and any future clients.
- **Voice lean:** Let the result speak. Brief context, then the quote or outcome.
- **Audience context:** Removes the final objection — proof that it works for real people.

### Engagement Content
Questions, polls, invitations to share. Learning from the audience.
- **Voice lean:** Curious and open. Genuinely interested in the answer.
- **Audience context:** Makes prospects feel heard and valued, not just marketed to.

## Audience Consciousness Level

The ideal 1-2-1 client sits between Courage (200) and Love (500) on David Hawkins' Map of Consciousness. Speak across the full band — primary focus Neutrality through Reason (250–400). Touch on Courage to meet those ready to act. Touch on Love to call forward those who sense something larger is possible.

**The emotional tone is frustration at the mechanics — not at themselves.** Why isn't this working? What am I missing? Not: what is wrong with me?

Personal pain — the realisation that significant time has passed without change — is used sparingly and deliberately. It lands precisely because it isn't constant.

**Voice implication:** Content is emotional but not pain-focused. Lead with emotional recognition (brief and real), follow with intellectual explanation. Energising and forward-facing. The reader feels recognised as capable and curious — not rescued.

## Audience Awareness

When writing for people who are trying to consciously create change but can't make it work:

- **Sophistication:** High — this audience has been in personal development for years. Don't over-explain the basics.
- **Use their language:** "I cannot make it work for me." / "I understand it but I can't feel it." / "Another week and nothing's changed." / "I know the old happy me is still inside me somewhere."
- **Address their pain:** The gap between knowing and feeling. The restart cycle. The quiet fear that they might be the problem.
- **Promise their transformation:** Living as the version of themselves they've been trying to become. Feeling it, not just knowing it. Creating consistently from the heart.

## Voice Matching Checklist

When creating any content for Olly Henson Coaching, verify:

- [ ] Sounds like Olly wrote it — conversational, not corporate
- [ ] Uses first person naturally
- [ ] Mixes short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones
- [ ] Bold and direct — states the argument clearly without hedging
- [ ] Includes personal context or story where relevant
- [ ] Emoji use is minimal and purposeful (0–2 max)
- [ ] British English spelling throughout
- [ ] Ends with an appropriate CTA (soft, never pushy)
- [ ] Does NOT use any banned words or phrases
- [ ] Does NOT sound like a press release or marketing copy
- [ ] Does NOT use em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators in captions
- [ ] Does NOT use a comma before 'and' or 'or'
- [ ] Does NOT assume knowledge the audience doesn't have in hooks — lead with what they've done or experienced, not what they know intellectually
- [ ] Appropriate for the target platform
