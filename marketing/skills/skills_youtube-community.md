## 1. Purpose

Write YouTube Community posts for @TheOllyHenson that build connection, trust and relatability with the target audience — people in the reality creation, manifestation and subconscious reprogramming niche who understand the theory but cannot bridge the gap to lived experience.

**Rachel is the primary avatar.** She has been in the personal development world for 5–20+ years. She's read the books, done the Dispenza meditations, said the affirmations, tried Neville Goddard. She believes transformation is possible. She cannot make it work in practice. She often carries unexplained physical symptoms or chronic illness alongside the emotional stuck-ness. And underneath everything is a layer of shame — not just frustration, but the quiet belief that she might be the problem.

Posts serve two goals:
1. Keep the audience warm and engaged between YouTube videos
2. Drive the right people to download the free Heart Activation Meditation or apply for The Heart Creator Program

## 2. Post Types

There are six post types. Always confirm which type is needed before writing.

### Type 1 — Personal Story
Olly shares a moment from his journey — chronic illness thread or conscious creation thread. The before/turning point/after arc. Most common and highest-performing type. See Section 4 for full structure.

**Two threads:**
- **Chronic illness thread** — post-concussion syndrome, the years of searching, nervous system locked in survival, Heart Coherence as the mechanism that allowed genuine healing
- **Conscious creation thread** — the 2018 $13k month, accidentally using Heart Coherence to embody the version of himself who could create that outcome, realising the mechanism works for creation not just healing

Both threads point to the same thing: unlocking the heart changes everything.

### Type 2 — Personal Update
A photo of what Olly is doing — a trip, an activity, something real in his life. Short caption. Shows he is living the life he talks about. Adds humanity and breaks the content rhythm.

### Type 3 — Engagement
A fun, light question for the audience — either a poll (4 options) or an open question. Always themed around the niche in a playful way rather than being business-relevant. Think: "What's your favourite reality creation movie?" or "Which Dispenza book changed you most?" — conversational, fun, builds community without selling. Short and direct. No preamble. End with the question — no sign-off needed.

### Type 4 — Personal Mission
Why Olly does this work. Connects to his own experience and the gap he sees between what the mainstream personal development world offers and what actually creates change. Belief-driven and values-led. Can reference the bigger mission: getting the world into its heart.

### Type 5 — Case Study
A client result — screenshot, interview clip or written story. Proof-based. Focuses entirely on the client's experience, not Olly's credentials. Let the result speak.

### Type 6 — Belief-Changing
Names a belief Rachel is trapped in — in her own words — validates it, reframes it logically, and shifts it. One belief per post. 150–250 words.

**Beliefs to target (drawn from real prospect and customer language):**
- "I understand it completely. I just can't feel it."
- "I've done everything right and nothing is changing"
- "I can sometimes feel it in meditation. But it never lasts."
- "I've been in this world for years. Why isn't it working for me?"
- "Everyone else seems to be able to feel this. I can't."
- "Maybe I'm just not capable of making this work"
- "Another week. And nothing has changed."
- "I know the old happy me is still inside me somewhere"
- "I keep getting glimpses. Then I crash right back."
- "I've spent years and thousands of dollars on this. Still on the same rung."
- "I go through the motions. But there's no joy in it."
- "I know it works for other people. I just can't make it work for me."
- "I'm not sure if it's that I can't feel it — or that I won't."

**Structure:**
```
HOOK — THE BELIEF
State the belief directly in Rachel's own words. Use quotation marks.
The quote must name a specific technique — visualise, meditate, affirm — not "do the work" generically.
Good: "I just need to visualise better and be more consistent."
Bad: "I just need to be more consistent."

> Note on Rachel's baseline: she may not be able to visualise at all — don't assume she can. Lead with effort and willpower, not visualisation or "feeling it vividly." Those are things she's trying and failing at, not things she can do naturally.

IDENTIFIER
Immediately after the hook: acknowledge you hear this — "I hear this a lot."
Then name the context in 1–2 lines so Rachel feels seen before the reframe begins.

VALIDATE
Meet her inside it. "If you've spent years trying things that haven't
lasted, of course you'd feel that way."
Never make her feel foolish for holding the belief.

REFRAME — use questions, not declarations (confirmed 2026-06-23)
Use "But what if..." questions to introduce the reframe. Questions feel like an invitation, not a lecture.
Good: "But what if it's nothing to do with the technique? What if it's the STATE you're doing it in?"
Never state the reframe as a flat declaration — Rachel arrives at it herself through the question.

SURVIVAL MODE — soften it
When naming survival mode as the cause, soften: "It's just because you're still mainly operating from a survival-based state."
Never make Rachel feel clinically assessed or blamed.

HEART LANGUAGE — not Heart Coherence (confirmed 2026-06-23)
In Belief-Changing posts, use "opening your heart" / "getting into your heart" language.
Do NOT name "Heart Coherence" here — that lives in Educational posts and deeper video content.
Keep it warm, felt and accessible.

CLOSE — plant the seed, no CTA (confirmed 2026-06-23)
Close with a forward-looking seed rather than a CTA. Rachel builds the belief gradually across posts.
Example: "Getting out of your head and into your heart is where this all starts."
Example: "Once you open your heart, this changes."
```

**Reframe technique — the learning loop:**
One of the most powerful reframe patterns is naming the loop Rachel is already in:
```
So you end up having to go and learn more, to still not be able to really feel it.

This pattern can go on for years.
```
This lands because it names something she recognises but has never seen named. Use it when the belief is about being stuck despite effort and knowledge.

**Reframe technique — one-sentence logic:**
The reframe itself should be a single clean logical sentence, not a science lesson.
```
Your subconscious mind only responds to feeling.
```
Not: "Your nervous system is in high beta and blocking access to your subconscious mind." That's a lecture. One sentence. Then let it land.

**Promised land — be specific:**
The close must name a specific experience Rachel will have, not a generic feeling better.
```
Approved: "Your imagination feels like a creative tool rather than something you have to force."
Rejected: "You'll finally be able to create the life you want."
```

**What makes it work:** the reframe must feel like an explanation, not a pep talk. Rachel needs to understand why — not be told to believe harder. The belief shift lands in the final line before the close.

## 3. Format Rules (apply to all post types)

- **Line length:** Short. One or two sentences per line maximum. Never long paragraphs.
- **White space:** Heavy. Every single line or phrase must be separated by a blank line — not just grouped paragraphs. This is critical for formatting when copied out. Even a one-liner gets its own blank line above and below.
- **Emoji usage:** Use where they fit naturally — not as a rule. A single relevant emoji can work at the top of a post (e.g. 💚 for a healing story). ➩ for lists. Never force them. Never mid-post unless it genuinely fits.
- **Arrow style for lists:** ➡️ — consistent throughout.
- **Ellipsis:** Use ... for pacing and dramatic rhythm, especially in personal story posts. "Back in 2018..." / "But that didn't stop me..."
- **No em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators** — use a full stop or comma instead.
- **No comma before 'and' or 'or'.**
- **Spelling:** British English throughout. Casual and real — "I didn't half arse it either" is fine.
- **Sign-off options:**
  - **Default close: "Olly 😎"** — confirmed 2026-06-23. This is the standard close for all YT Community Posts
  - Casual (gifting something): "Enjoy 😎" — when the post ends on an invitation or gifts something (e.g. dropping a free resource in the comments)
  - Warm close: "Enjoy, Olly" — good for posts that end with a gift
  - Soft (occasional variation): "To your health, happiness and success. Olly"
  - Reflective close: a warm final line that fits the story — e.g. "I hope you do too. Olly"
  - Engagement close: end with the question — no sign-off needed

## 4. Personal Story Structure

Use this arc for all Type 1 posts:

```
HOOK
Open with a specific, grounded fact — a number, a date, a real situation.
Not a vague experiential statement like "Something shifted" or "I was just different."

Approved: "In November and December 2018... My income grew by 364%."
Rejected: "Back in 2018... Something shifted."

The hook must give the reader something concrete to hold onto before they've
even clicked. Vague opening lines lose people above the fold.

SETUP
Brief context. What was the real situation? What was at stake?
Keep it short — 2–4 lines. Ground it in specifics: where Olly was living,
what was happening, what he needed. Not just mood — facts.
Include the specific life circumstances that created the pressure:
housing situation, financial reality, a recent event (return from travel,
an injury, a job loss). These details are what make the circumstances feel
real rather than constructed. "I was back living with my parents at 28.
I'd just returned from travelling Europe. I'd taken a serious head injury
in September that was affecting my ability to work." — each line adds
a layer that grounds the story in a real moment.

SYMPTOMS / WHAT IT LOOKED LIKE
List using ➡️ arrows. 5–8 specific experiences. Lead with what Rachel
will recognise — exhaustion, brain fog, emotional reactivity, the feeling
of going through the motions, the inspired state that always crashes,
the gap between knowing and actually feeling it.
Do not lead with "chronic illness" as the hook — lead with the specific
experience.

TURNING POINT
What changed. What Olly realised, discovered, or did differently.
"What I realised was..." / "Things only changed when..." / "So I changed direction."

THE MECHANISM (where relevant)
Heart Coherence, HeartMath, nervous system regulation — introduce naturally,
not as a sales pitch. Always "something called Heart Coherence" on first mention.
Olly became a certified HeartMath Practitioner.

THE RESULT
What life looks like now. Specific and grounded — not vague wellness promises.
For the chronic illness thread: getting back to driving, working, socialising,
his wedding day, concerts.
For the conscious creation thread: the $13k month, embodying the version of
himself who could create that outcome, the life that followed.

CLOSE
Either a CTA, a soft sign-off, or an engagement question. See Section 6.
```

## 5. Writing Style — Patterns From Live Posts

Content examples will be added as reality creation posts are written and approved. Until then, the following style patterns are extracted from live posts and must be replicated exactly.

### Sentence Length
One sentence per line. Always. Occasionally two very short sentences on one line — but never more. If a sentence is long, break it.

### Rhythm and Ellipsis
Ellipsis mid-post creates breathing points that keep the reader scrolling — not just at the end of sentences but as standalone transition lines:

```
But nothing moved the needle...

Confused and deflated...

But here's another way I'd like you to look at this...
```

These are not decoration. They are the pacing mechanism. Use them throughout the body of the post wherever the reader needs a moment before the next point lands.

### Paragraph Structure
Single line. Blank line. Single line. Blank line. Relentless throughout. No blocks of text anywhere in the post — not even in the close.

### Lists
- ✅ Green checkbox emoji for positive outcomes and results — what life looks like after the shift
- ➡️ Arrow for neutral lists — symptoms, experiences, steps
- Plain single lines for the build-up and argument — no emoji needed
- Never mix list styles in the same section

### Transition Lines
Short pivots that reset the reader's attention before a new point:
```
Now, here's the truth...

But here's another way I'd like you to look at this...

Things changed considerably, when...

The day things turned around:
```

Use these to move between setup, turning point and mechanism — they signal a shift without breaking the rhythm.

### The Close
Always soft. An invitation, not a pitch. Never urgent. Never pushy.
```
Just let me know in the comments below and I can point you in the right direction.

I'll leave some info in the comments below for you.

Check out the link in the comments below:
```

The CTA is always in the comments — not in the post body itself. The post ends with the invitation line, then the link goes in the pinned comment.

### Overall Tone
Confessional and direct. Reads like someone talking, not writing. Never polished. The reader should feel like Olly is speaking to them personally, not broadcasting to an audience.

## 7. Tone Rules

- Write as Olly — first person, honest, direct, never preachy
- Meet Rachel inside her experience before moving to the solution
- Never promise a result. The frame is: *I found something that worked for me — here it is*
- Never be clinical. Plain language throughout — "survival mode", "body felt safe", "the feeling", "going through the motions"
- Never sound like a sales pitch or a wellness influencer
- No influencer-energy phrasing: "will change everything you thought you knew", "game changer", "life-changing"
- The Heart Coherence mechanism should feel like a discovery Olly made, not a product he's selling
- British English throughout — no exceptions
- Write as if speaking directly to one person who has been trying for a long time and cannot figure out why it is not working
- Frame the solution around getting into the heart and feeling — not the mechanics of Heart Coherence. "When you get out of your head... when you regulate your nervous system... and get into your heart... your ability to feel will actually return." That is more felt than "Heart Coherence turns off survival mode."

### Never
- Use banned words: game changer, deep dive, synergy, leverage (as verb), hustle, grind, low-hanging fruit, unlock, skyrocket, supercharge
- Use clickbait or over-promise
- Lead with "chronic illness" as the hook — lead with the specific experience instead
- Assume Rachel knows what Heart Coherence is — always introduce it as "something called Heart Coherence"
- Use em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators
- Use a comma before 'and' or 'or'
- Sound dismissive of the tools Rachel has already tried — they worked partially. The problem is they were missing the foundation
- Open a Personal Story with a vague experiential statement — "Something shifted" or "I was just different" loses people above the fold. Open with a specific fact: a number, a date, a real situation
- Assume Rachel can visualise — she may not be able to at all. Lead with effort and willpower as the universal experience, not visualisation or "feeling it vividly." Those are things she's trying and failing at, not things she does naturally
- Write a pinned comment that names only the mechanism — always name the problem Rachel has first, then the outcome she'll get. "Experience Heart Coherence" is not an outcome. "Feel like the world you're trying to create is actually building around you" is
- Over-explain the pinned comment — the post body carries the emotional context; the pinned comment just needs to be direct and easy to click. "Download the free Heart Activation Meditation here:" beats a long sentence every time
- Write a standard meditation CTA that is generic — name where Rachel is right now. "If you'd like to experience Heart Coherence" speaks to everyone. "If you're currently struggling to see results and really feel like your future is already happening" speaks to Rachel specifically
- Write a Personal Story setup that is only emotional — include the specific life circumstances (housing, finances, health, a recent event) that created the pressure. Details like "back living with my parents at 28", "I'd just returned from travelling Europe", "I'd taken a serious head injury" are what make a real moment feel real, not constructed
- Use a mechanistic science explanation as the reframe in a Belief-Changing post — one clean logical sentence lands harder than a paragraph of neuroscience
- Name "Heart Coherence" in a Belief-Changing post — use "opening your heart" / "getting into your heart" language. Heart Coherence belongs in Educational posts
- State the Belief-Changing reframe as a flat declaration — use "But what if..." questions so Rachel arrives at the conclusion herself
- Add a CTA to any post except the first post of the week on Monday — all other posts end without a CTA or Pinned Comment
- Link the Monday CTA to the meditation — Monday CTA goes to the Heart Creator Program only
- Use the same fold word twice in a row — rotate But / Yet / Until / Still / Except / So across posts
- Use the fold word technique in email or Instagram Stories — YouTube Community Posts only
- Write a Belief-Changing opening quote without a specific technique — always name visualise, meditate, affirm, or a practice Rachel will recognise

### The Title Field — Above-the-Fold Hook

The Title field is **not a title**. It is the first 1–2 lines of the post — what YouTube shows before "Read more". These lines must create enough curiosity that someone clicks to read the rest.

**The rule:** YouTube truncates after roughly 1–2 short lines. Everything above the fold must do one job: make the reader need to know what comes next.

**Three proven approaches (from live posts):**

**1. Two-line build with ellipsis**
The first line is incomplete — it lands the second line as a gut punch.
```
Another week...

And nothing's changed.
```
Why it works: the ellipsis creates a pause. The second line names the pain Rachel already feels. She clicks because she recognises it.

**2. Prospect quote in quotation marks**
Opens with something Rachel has thought or said herself, then Olly responds.
```
"I know healing is possible, I just can't make it work for me."

That's something I hear a lot.
```
Why it works: the quote does the recognition work instantly. "That's something I hear a lot" validates without explaining — she clicks to find out what Olly says next.

**3. Unfinished open question**
A question that trails off with an ellipsis — the answer is below the fold.
```
Have you ever noticed that every time you try something new to feel better...
```
Why it works: the unfinished sentence is a pattern interrupt. The brain needs the rest of it. She has to click.

**The fold word technique:**
End the above-the-fold section with a fold word on its own as the final word before "Read more." Rotate the fold word across posts — never use the same word twice in a row.
Approved fold words: **But / Yet / Until / Still / Except / So**
Character target: ~150–155 characters in the Title field so the fold word lands right before YouTube cuts to "Read more."
This technique is YouTube Community Posts only — do not use in email or Instagram Stories.

**Rules for the hook:**
- Maximum 2 lines above the fold
- Always end on an ellipsis, a gut-punch statement, or an unfinished thought
- Must speak directly to Rachel's lived experience — not a concept, not a question she'd have to think about
- No complete thoughts above the fold — leave something unresolved
- The hook field in Notion = the full post body. The Title field = just the above-the-fold opening lines

**When creating a new entry:**

2. Present to Olly for review
3. Wait for confirmation before pushing to Notion

## 9. Process — Writing a Post

1. **Confirm post type** — Personal Story / Personal Update / Engagement / Mission / Case Study / Belief-Changing
2. **Get the hook or context** — what's the specific moment, photo, result, or question?
3. **For Personal Story posts:** confirm which thread — chronic illness or conscious creation. Follow the arc in Section 4
4. **Draft Image Idea** — what image or photo would accompany this post?
5. **Apply format rules** — short lines, heavy white space, arrow lists where relevant
6. **Choose the right close** — standard meditation CTA, coaching application CTA, engagement question, or soft sign-off. Vary across personal stories to avoid the constant-pitch pattern
7. **Draft Pinned Comment** — the UTM link that goes in comments after publishing (meditation or coaching application)
8. **Check tone** — read it back as Olly. Does it sound like him? Would he be comfortable posting this?
9. **Check constraints** — no banned words, British English, no over-promise, no em-dashes
