## 2. Post Types

Use the same six-type system as other Heart Creator content. Confirm which type is needed before writing.

- **Personal Story** - a moment from Olly's journey (chronic illness thread or the $13k conscious creation thread). Before / turning point / after arc. See Section 4.
- **Personal Update** - behind-the-scenes, a real moment, workspace, an activity. Short. Shows Olly is living the life he talks about.
- **Engagement** - a fun, light, niche-themed poll or open question. Conversational, not business-relevant. e.g. "What's your favourite reality creation movie?"
- **Personal Mission** - why Olly does this work. Belief-driven, connects to the bigger mission of getting the world into its heart.
- **Case Study** - a client result (screenshot, quote, written story). Proof-based, focused on the client's experience.
- **Belief-Changing** - names a belief Rachel is trapped in, validates it, reframes it. One belief per Story sequence. See Section 3.

## 3. Belief-Changing Structure

```
HOOK - THE BELIEF
State the belief in Rachel's own words. Name a specific technique she's tried
(visualise, meditate, affirm) - not "do the work" generically.
Good: "I just need to visualise better and be more consistent."
Note: Rachel may not be able to visualise at all - lead with effort and willpower, not visualisation.

VALIDATE
Meet her inside it before reframing. "If you've spent years trying things that haven't
lasted, of course you'd feel that way." Never make her feel foolish for holding the belief.

REFRAME - use questions, not declarations
"But what if..." questions land better than flat statements - she arrives at the reframe herself.
Good: "But what if it's nothing to do with the technique? What if it's the STATE you're doing it in?"

The reframe itself should be one clean logical sentence, not a science lecture:
"Your subconscious mind only responds to feeling."

SURVIVAL MODE - soften it
"It's just because you're still mainly operating from a survival-based state." Never clinical or blaming.

HEART LANGUAGE - not "Heart Coherence" at this stage
Use "opening your heart" / "getting into your heart" - warm and accessible, not mechanistic.

CLOSE - plant the seed or CTA
A forward-looking line, or the HEART/COMMUNITY CTA depending on where this Story sits in the sequence.
```

**Reframe pattern - the learning loop:** name the loop she's already stuck in - "So you end up having to go and learn more, to still not be able to really feel it. This pattern can go on for years." Use when the belief is about being stuck despite effort and knowledge.

**Promised land - be specific, not generic:**
Approved: "Your imagination feels like a creative tool rather than something you have to force."
Rejected: "You'll finally be able to create the life you want."

## 4. Personal Story Arc

```
HOOK - proof-first, not vague
Approved: "In November and December 2018... My income grew by 364%."
Rejected: "Back in 2018... Something shifted."

SETUP - concrete specifics, not just mood
Ground it in real circumstances: housing, finances, a recent event (travel, injury, job loss).
"I was back living with my parents at 28. I'd just returned from travelling Europe.
I'd taken a serious head injury in September that was affecting my ability to work."

SYMPTOMS / WHAT IT LOOKED LIKE
5-8 specific, recognisable experiences - exhaustion, brain fog, the inspired state that
always crashes, the gap between knowing and feeling it. Don't lead with the diagnosis label.

TURNING POINT
"What I realised was..." / "Things only changed when..."

THE MECHANISM
Introduce Heart Coherence naturally, not as a pitch. Always "something called Heart Coherence" on first mention.

THE RESULT
Specific and grounded, not vague wellness promises.

CLOSE
HEART/COMMUNITY CTA, soft sign-off, or engagement question.
```

## 5. Format Rules

Instagram Stories are a series of vertical slides. Each slide is short - the reader taps through.

- **Slide length:** 1-3 lines of text per slide maximum
- **Behind-the-scenes:** workspace, process, real moments for Personal Update posts
- **Polls & questions:** use native Instagram stickers for Engagement posts
- **Quick tips:** one actionable idea spread across 2-3 slides
- **Social proof:** testimonials, client wins, DM screenshots (with permission) for Case Study posts
- Post 3-7 stories per day when active
- No em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators - use a full stop or comma
- No comma before 'and' or 'or'
- British English throughout

## 7. What Never to Write

- Never fabricate client results - only use real proof from Isabell and Anas (and any future clients)
- Never use banned words: game changer, deep dive, synergy, leverage, hustle, grind, unlock, skyrocket, supercharge
- Never assume Rachel knows what Heart Coherence is - always "something called Heart Coherence" on first mention
- Never assume Rachel can visualise - lead with effort and willpower as the universal experience
- Never open a Personal Story with a vague experiential statement - open with a specific fact, number, or date
- Never name "Heart Coherence" in a Belief-Changing Story - use "opening your heart" / "getting into your heart"
- Never state the Belief-Changing reframe as a flat declaration - use "But what if..." questions
- Never include a destination URL directly in a Story - ManyChat handles delivery
- Never use em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators
- Never use a comma before 'and' or 'or'
- Never let a CTA keyword stay named after a retired offer — when the offer changes, rename the keyword to match it.
