# Thumbnail Photo & Expression Catalogue — Olly Henson Coaching

> A complete brief for one focused photo session. Capture everything here and you'll have a full asset library to draw from for months.
> Target: 60–80 usable shots across all categories. Takes roughly 60–90 minutes.

---

## Setup Before You Shoot

**Location:** Anywhere with a clean, uncluttered background — plain wall, dark wall, or even outdoors with a blurred background. The background is replaced in Canva anyway, but cleaner shots are easier to work with.

**Lighting:** Natural window light is ideal. Position yourself facing the window (light on your face, not behind you). One direction only — avoid overhead lights.

**Camera:** iPhone in portrait mode is fine. Set up on a tripod or lean it against something at chest height, angled very slightly upward. Use the self-timer (3 or 10 seconds) or have someone click for you.

**What to wear:** 2–3 outfit changes. Stick to dark tops (black, charcoal, dark navy) — they don't compete with the thumbnail background. Avoid patterns, logos, or anything distracting.

**Frame:** Shoot from chest/collarbone up. Not a full headshot crop — you want enough of your body to feel present, not just a floating head.

---

## Expression Categories

Shoot 8–12 shots per category. You don't need to think about which template each goes in — just hit the expression and shoot multiples. You'll pick the best ones after.

---

### 1. Quiet Certainty
*The "I know something you don't" look.*

- Calm, still face
- Slight downward angle of the eyes — not looking at camera, looking slightly past it
- Mouth closed or very slightly open
- No forced anything — just settled

**Used for hooks like:** "Feel it first" / "Your heart knows" / "The body remembers"

**Bonus shot:** Same expression but with one hand resting on your chest (heart). Very on-brand.

---

### 2. Mid-Thought Intensity
*Caught mid-sentence. The most versatile expression.*

- Looking slightly off to one side (left or right — shoot both)
- Slight lean forward, like you're about to make a point
- Eyes focused, engaged — not dramatic, just present
- Mouth can be slightly open as if mid-word

**Used for hooks like:** "This changes everything" / "Not a mind game" / "I went all in"

---

### 3. Pattern Interrupt / "Wait for it"
*The raised-eyebrow, mild-surprise look.*

- Direct to camera
- One eyebrow slightly raised or both eyes slightly wider than normal
- Not shock — more like "you're going to want to hear this"
- Can have a very slight knowing smile — but not a full grin

**Used for hooks like:** "Too easy" / "Try it right now" / "Do this tonight"

---

### 4. Relaxed Confidence
*Arms crossed or hands in pockets. Unhurried.*

- Direct to camera or slight angle
- Slight smirk — not a smile, just easy confidence
- Head very slightly tilted (5–10 degrees) — more human, less posed
- Body language: relaxed, grounded, not performing

**Used for hooks like:** "Still in your head?" / "You're doing it wrong" / "Most people won't"

---

### 5. Soft Vulnerability
*The personal story look.*

- Eyes slightly down or to the side — introspective, not sad
- Softer jaw, no tension
- Could be in mid-thought about something personal
- Optional: slight nod forward, like you're remembering something

**Used for hooks like:** "Nothing was working" / "Then everything shifted" / "I didn't believe it"

---

### 6. Mission Energy
*Strong, direct, a step above normal.*

- Direct to camera, chin very slightly up
- Eyes sharp and clear
- Not aggressive — purposeful
- You know why you do this work. This is that face.

**Used for hooks like:** "The world needs this" / "Heart over head" / "Fear runs the world"

---

### 7. Genuine Surprise / Realisation
*Not performed shock — actual "wait, this is bigger than I thought" energy.*

- Eyes slightly wider than normal
- Slight drop of the jaw — natural, not exaggerated
- One hand can come up toward the face or chin
- Looking slightly upward or at camera

**Used for hooks like:** "I went all in" / "It took one moment" / "Watch what happens"

---

## Props to Shoot With

Shoot 5–10 shots with each prop. These are for Template 4 (three-zone layout) and any video where a visual element adds meaning.

| Prop | What it communicates | How to shoot it |
|------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Hand on chest | Heart coherence, feeling, the heart as the mechanism | Close crop of hand on chest, or face + hand in same shot |
| Open journal / notebook | Personal story, reflection, the written intention | Hold it open, looking down at it or looking up from it |
| Pen in hand | Clarity, writing down intention | Mid-writing or holding pen up thoughtfully |
| Looking at phone screen | Content idea for "what most people do" contrast | Looking at screen, then looking up |
| Nothing — just pointing | Direct, conversational, "this thing I'm about to tell you" | Pointing slightly toward camera |

---

## After the Session

**Editing:** Keep edits minimal — consistent warmth/colour grade across all shots. You want them to feel like the same person in the same universe, not 7 different photoshoots.

**Culling:** Go through and star your top 2–3 per expression category. That gives you 14–21 hero shots.

**Uploading to Canva:**
1. Go to YouTube Thumbnails → Assets folder
2. Create a subfolder called "Face Shots"
3. Upload your selected photos
4. They'll be ready to drag straight into any template
