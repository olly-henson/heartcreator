# YouTube Thumbnail Brief — Olly Henson Coaching

> One-pager for producing consistent, high-performing thumbnails fast.
> Inspired by Chris 'The Wiz' Miller's style, translated into the Olly Henson brand.

---

## The Formula

**Face + Dark Background + 2–4 Word Hook**

That's it. No busy design. No gradients over the face. No borders. The power is in the simplicity — it looks like a moment, not a marketing asset.

---

## Specs

- **Size:** 1280 x 720px (Canva template)
- **Background:** Space Black `#080010` or Deep Purple `#1a0535` — or the Stars Background asset for a cosmic feel
- **Text colour:** Star White `#f8f4ff` for the main hook. Magenta `#d946ef` for a single accent word if needed
- **Font:** Mont Bold for the hook text. Large enough to read at mobile thumbnail size

---

## Layout

```
+------------------------------------------+
|                                          |
|   [HOOK TEXT]          [YOUR FACE]       |
|   2–4 words            Large, left or    |
|   top-left or          right third       |
|   top-centre           of frame          |
|                                          |
|                                          |
+------------------------------------------+
```

- Face takes up roughly 40–50% of the frame
- Text is large — if you can read it comfortably at full size, make it bigger
- Leave breathing room — do not fill every corner
- Occasionally: one prop or overlay (a handwritten note, a diagram, a visual symbol). Never more than one extra element

---

## Expression Direction

This is the most important part. Chris's thumbnails work because his face looks *real*.

**What works:**
- Mid-thought — slightly looking off-camera, like you're in the middle of saying something
- Quiet intensity — calm but certain. The expression of someone who knows something you don't
- Genuine surprise or realisation — eyes slightly wide, natural
- Relaxed confidence — not performing, just present

**What to avoid:**
- Big open-mouth shock (looks clickbait-y)
- Forced smile or "coaching pose"
- Looking directly at camera with a staged expression

**Practical tip:** Take 20–30 photos after filming while you're still in the energy of the topic. You'll get something real. Don't use a separate "thumbnail photo session" — it rarely looks natural.

## Hook Text Bank

Organised by content pillar. Pull one when you need it — or use these as a starting point and riff.

### Argument-Based (Why it's not working)
- Still in your head?
- You're doing it wrong
- This is why it fails
- It's not information
- The missing piece
- You're not broken
- Not a mind game

### Educational (Heart Coherence / mechanism)

- Your heart knows
- Get out of your head

- It's not a thought
- This changes everything
- The real reason why

### Personal Story

- This healed me
- It took one moment
- I didn't believe it
- Nothing was working
- Then everything shifted

### Mission / Bigger Picture
- Most people won't
- The world needs this
- This is why I do it
- Fear runs the world
- Heart over head

### Curiosity / Pattern Interrupt
- Too easy
- Try it right now
- You already know this
- Watch what happens
- Do this tonight
- Just feel it

## Text Formatting Rules

- **All caps or title case** — both work. All caps for short punchy hooks (3 words or fewer). Title case for slightly longer hooks
- **No more than two lines** — if it needs a third line, cut a word
- **One accent word in magenta** — optional, but effective for emphasis (e.g., "The **REAL** Reason Why")
- **No subtitle text on the thumbnail** — let the video title do that job

## Canva Workflow (10 minutes or less)

1. Open your Olly Henson YouTube Thumbnail template in Canva (1280 x 720px)
2. Drop in your chosen photo — position face right or left third
3. Set background to Space Black or Stars Background asset
4. Add hook text in Playfair Display Bold, Star White, large
5. Optionally: add one magenta accent word
6. Export as JPG — done

**Brand assets in Canva to have ready:**
- Stars Background (PNG from assets folder)
- Olly pfp / face photos (keep a folder of good expression shots)
- Colour palette saved as brand kit
- Playfair Display saved as brand font

## Quick Reference — Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Dark background, face prominent | Bright busy backgrounds |
| 2–4 words, bold and large | Full sentences or questions |
| Real expression, mid-thought | Staged coaching smile |
| One accent colour if needed | Multiple colours or gradients over face |
| Breathing room in the layout | Cramming text into every corner |
| Match the energy of the video | Generic "surprised face" every time |
