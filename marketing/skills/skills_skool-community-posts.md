## 1. Purpose

Write posts for the Heart Creator Skool community that support members in learning, practising and mastering Heart Coherence as part of their reality creation journey.

Members are in the reality creation, manifestation and subconscious reprogramming niche. They have joined to go deeper than free content — they want the practice, not just the theory. Rachel is the primary avatar.

2. Educate and normalise the experience of practising Heart Coherence
3. Subtly move the right people towards The Heart Creator Program (1-2-1 coaching — $5,000)

## 2. Posting Rhythm

Post every weekday as a guide. Read the room — skip a day if the community feels overwhelmed or if there is nothing genuinely useful to say. Never post for the sake of posting.

## 3. Post Types

There are six post types. Confirm which type is needed before writing.

### Type 1 — Inspirational
Keep members motivated when progress feels slow. Olly shares from his own experience. Peer-to-peer in tone — not coach-to-student. No sell. The frame is: *I've been where you are, keep going.*

### Type 2 — Engaging
A check-in or question — either an open question or a poll. Short and direct. Purpose is genuine connection and feedback, not content for content's sake. Two subtypes:
- **Check-in:** How are you? What are you working on? What do you need?
- **Poll:** 4-option multiple choice. Can be personal (about their experience) or professional (about their condition or symptoms). Keep options simple and relatable.

### Type 3 — Technical
Addresses a difficulty or objection that commonly comes up around Heart Coherence practice — particularly issues that surface during live calls. Never frames it as "someone struggled with this." Always framed generally: *"If you've ever noticed..."* Normalise the difficulty, explain why it happens, give the path forward.

### Type 4 — Client / Call Wins
Celebrates results from current paid clients or attendance and breakthroughs from the weekly live Heart Coherence calls. Specific, grounded, never exaggerated. Screenshot or paraphrase with permission. Focus on the member's experience, not Olly's credentials. *(Examples to be added — Olly will supply these as they come in.)*

### Type 5 — Call Update
Announces the upcoming weekly live Heart Coherence call. Time zones, what members will learn and feel, soft engagement CTA. **Use `skills/skills_skool-live-call-post.md` for the full template and gold standard reference for this post type.**

## 4. Format Rules (all post types)

- **Line length:** Short. One or two sentences per line maximum.
- **White space:** Heavy. Blank line between every line. Skool reads like a chat feed.
- **Rhythm:** Ellipsis (...) is used to create a slow, reading-pace rhythm — particularly in Inspirational and Technical posts. Use it naturally, not mechanically.
- **Emoji usage:** Minimal across most post types — 0 to 1 emoji per post.
  - Exception: Hand Raise posts use ✅ for the benefit checklist.
  - Exception: Call Update posts follow the emoji rules in `skills_skool-live-call-post.md`.
- **Spelling:** British English throughout — no exceptions.
- **Sign-off options:**
  - Warm close: `Olly` or `To your health, happiness and success. Olly`
  - Engagement close: end with a question — no sign-off needed
  - Technical close: `Any questions?` — invites follow-up without pressure
- **Banned words:** game changer, deep dive, synergy, leverage (as verb), hustle, grind, low-hanging fruit, unlock, skyrocket, supercharge (see `CLAUDE.md`)

---

## 5. Tone Rules

- Write as Olly — first person, honest, warm, never preachy
- Peer framing, not expert-to-patient — *"I know the darkness well"*, *"I will be here for you"*
- Meet members inside their experience before moving to the solution
- Never promise a cure or overnight results — the frame is always gradual, consistent, real
- Plain language: "survival mode", "body feels safe", "find the light again", "heart comes back online"
- Never clinical, never wellness-influencer, never a sales pitch
- The community already knows the story — no need to over-explain HeartMath or the mechanism on every post

## 6. Post Structures

### Type 1 — Inspirational Structure

```
TITLE
Short, direct — a statement or incomplete thought that pulls the reader in.
E.g. "Don't Give Up...Yet"

OLLY'S MOMENT
One photo or specific moment from his own experience. Brief context — what was happening?

THE PAIN (mirroring)
What chronic illness does to a person beyond the physical — the numbness, the loss of joy,
the disconnection. This is where members feel seen.

THE TURN
What started to change. Heart Coherence, consistent practice, finding the light again.

HONEST CAVEAT
It takes time and consistent practice. No false promises. This builds trust.

CLOSE
A personal commitment to be there for them. Warm, not performative.
E.g. "I will be here for you as you do that."
```

### Type 2 — Engaging Structure

```
TITLE
Simple and direct. E.g. "How are you?"

OPENER
"Hey..." — casual, no preamble.

THE QUESTION(S)
1 to 3 short questions. What are they working on? What do they need? How are they feeling?
For polls: 4 options, simple language, relatable to where they actually are.

No sign-off needed — the question is the close.
```

### Type 3 — Technical Structure

```
TITLE
➡️ What To Do When [the difficulty]
The arrow emoji in the title is the convention for technical posts — it signals practical help.

OPEN WITH THE EXPERIENCE
"If you've tried... / If you've ever noticed..." — broad, general, never "someone on the call."

NORMALISE
"That's absolutely normal." Say it directly. Members need to hear this.

EXPLAIN WHY (simply)
Body in protective mode, armour, survival state — plain language, no jargon.

THE PATH FORWARD
What consistent Heart Coherence practice does to address this. Gradual, honest.

THE RESULT
What it feels like when the body starts to let go. Specific and grounded.

CLOSE
"Any questions?" — opens the door without pressure.
```

### Example 2 — Technical ("What To Do When The Body Feels Tight & Restrictive")
Title: *➡️ What To Do When The Body Feels Tight & Restrictive*
Difficulty addressed: Body feeling tight or stiff during Heart Coherence or any mindful practice
Mechanism explained: Body in protective mode ("armour"), survival state
Path forward: Consistent daily practice signals safety; gradual release follows
Close: "Any questions?"

**Why this works:** Framed broadly ("if you've tried any meditation...") so no one feels called out. Normalises immediately. The "armour" metaphor is concrete and non-clinical. Ends with an open invitation rather than a lecture.

> You are letting your body know, time and time again - that it's safe.

> The better you'll begin to feel.

### Example 3 — Engaging (check-in)
Title: *How are you?*
Format: Three short questions. No preamble beyond "Hey...". No sign-off.

**Why this works:** Simplicity signals genuine interest. Members in chronic illness often feel unseen — a direct "how are you?" carries real weight. The follow-up questions about implementation and support make it actionable, not just performative.

**Full post:**
> How are you?
>
> Hey...
>
> So checking in with how you are...
>
> What are you implementing at the moment in your recovery?
>
> Anything you need support with right now?

## 8. Process — Writing a Post

1. **Confirm post type** — Inspirational / Engaging / Technical / Hand Raise / Client Wins / Call Update
2. **For Call Update posts** — use `skills/skills_skool-live-call-post.md` instead of this file
3. **For Technical posts** — ask Olly what difficulty or objection came up on the last live call
4. **For Client/Call Wins posts** — get the specific result or moment from Olly; never fabricate
5. **Draft the post** using the relevant structure in Section 6
7. **Apply format rules** — short lines, heavy white space, ellipsis rhythm where appropriate
8. **Check tone** — does it sound like Olly? Warm, honest, peer-level, no hype
9. **Check constraints** — no banned words, British English, no over-promise
