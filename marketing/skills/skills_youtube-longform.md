## 1. Purpose

Write YouTube long-form video entries for @TheOllyHenson — ideas, titles, hooks, outcomes, descriptions and pinned comments.

**Channel:** @TheOllyHenson
**Posting frequency:** 3x/week
**Format:** 10–20 minute talking head videos
**Target audience:** Rachel — someone in the reality creation, manifestation and subconscious reprogramming niche who understands the theory but cannot make it work in practice. She has tried Dispenza, Neville Goddard, affirmations, visualisation. She believes change is possible. She cannot feel it.

Read `memory/argument_sheet.md` and `memory/avatar-rachel.md` before generating any content.

## 2. Video Types

There are three video types. The majority of videos will be Belief-Changing while the channel is building an audience and accumulating client stories.

---

### Type 1 — Belief-Changing

The core format. Opens with a belief Rachel is stuck in — named in her own words — then builds the argument for why that belief is wrong, brings in the science to explain the mechanism, and lands the reframe. Personal stories (Olly's $13k month, chronic illness journey) and client proof drop in as the proof layer when available, not as a separate format.

**The structure:**
```
HOOK
Open with the belief Rachel holds — her exact words or a very close version.
Make her feel immediately seen. She should think "that's exactly how I feel."

THE ARGUMENT
Build the case for why this belief is wrong.
Name what she's been doing. Validate the effort. Then explain why it hasn't worked.
"It's not the technique — it's the state."

THE SCIENCE
Explain the mechanism in plain language.
Subconscious mind, fight/flight, brainwaves, Heart Coherence.
This is not a lecture — it's the explanation Rachel has been waiting for.
Keep it grounded in her experience, not in textbook language.

PROOF LAYER (when available)
Drop in a personal story or client result that makes the argument real.
Olly's $13k month. The chronic illness recovery. A client win.
This is not a separate segment — it flows naturally as "here's how I know this is true."

THE REFRAME
Land the new belief. Clean, simple, one idea.
"Transformation is not a head game. It is an experience game."
Leave her with something she didn't have when she clicked play.
```

**Personal story integration:**
Both of Olly's threads serve as proof inside this format:
- **Chronic illness thread** — nervous system locked in survival, Heart Coherence as the mechanism for healing
- **Conscious creation thread** — the 2018 $13k month, accidentally using Heart Coherence to embody the version of himself who could create that outcome

Always introduce Heart Coherence as "something called Heart Coherence" on first mention.

### Type 2 — Practical

Takes Rachel through an actual practice — a meditation or a specific exercise — inside the video. She does it, not just learns about it. This is the primary differentiator from every other creator in the niche who only explains things.

**Practices this format covers:**
- Heart-Focused Breathing and Heart Coherence
- Nervous system regulation exercises
- Exercises for feeling the future as real
- Guided meditations (shorter, in-video versions)
- Quick Coherence technique

**The structure:**
```
HOOK
Open with Rachel's frustration — the specific feeling this practice addresses.
Not the technique name. Her experience.

THE CASE FOR THIS PRACTICE
Brief argument for why this practice is the answer to that frustration.
Keep it short — she came here to do something, not to be sold to.

THE WALKTHROUGH
Step-by-step, in real time. Olly guides her through it as if she is in the room.
Slow. Specific. Embodied. She follows along.

THE DEBRIEF
Name what she may have noticed in her body during the practice.
Reframe any resistance or difficulty — it is the old programme, not failure.
Connect what she just felt back to the argument.

CTA
The meditation download and coaching application.
```

**Key rules for Practical videos:**
- The title names Rachel's frustration or desired outcome — not the technique
- The description para 3 must flag that a live practice is in the video
- The Outcome field covers what Rachel *did and felt*, not just what she now understands

---

### Type 3 — Client Success

Proof-led. Either Olly explains a client's result directly to camera, or he interviews the client. The client's experience is the entire focus — not Olly's credentials or the programme mechanics.

**Two formats:**
1. **Olly explains** — he walks through what the client came in with, what shifted, and what their life looks like now. Specific and grounded. No embellishment.
2. **Client interview** — the client speaks for themselves. Olly's role is to draw out the specific moments of change, not to summarise or interpret.

**The structure (Olly explains):**
```
HOOK
Name the specific result — not "my client had an amazing transformation."
Something concrete: a symptom gone, a relationship changed, an income created.

THE BEFORE
Where the client was. What they had tried. What wasn't working.
Make it recognisable to Rachel — she should see herself in this.

THE SHIFT
What changed. When it changed. The specific practice or moment.

THE AFTER
What their life looks like now. Specific details, not vague outcomes.

THE BRIDGE TO RACHEL
Connect the client's result back to the argument.
"The reason this worked for [name] is the same reason it will work for you."

CTA
```

## 3. Notion Entry Standards

Every entry must meet this standard before moving to Scripted.

### Title

- Ask: what is Rachel searching for on YouTube right now? Write the title as the answer to that search
- Name a felt, daily experience she recognises — not a concept, not a statistic
- **9 words maximum for the core title** — parentheticals do not count toward the limit. "(copy my state)", "(here's why)", "(use your heart)" can be appended as a second hook after the core title
- The statistic or science goes inside the video as proof — never in the title
- **The title must do two things:** (a) name the specific felt experience Rachel recognises, and (b) signal it is about reality creation. If a cold viewer cannot tell this is about creating their reality from the title alone, rewrite it
- Test every word: would Rachel use that word to search for this?
- **Default to aspirational.** Before writing a pain-led title, ask whether an outcome-led version performs better. "How to feel your future as real" outperforms "Why you can't feel your future as real" for the same argument. Pain-led is a deliberate choice, not the default
- **Aspirational title test:** Could this title appear on a Dispenza, Neville Goddard, or Abraham Hicks video and feel at home?

**High-performing title patterns from the niche:**

| Pattern | Example from niche | Olly version |
|---|---|---|
| Contrarian / pattern interrupt | "When I stopped visualising, I manifested EVERYTHING" | "When I stopped trying to feel it, everything changed" |
| First-person "I" discovery | "I Found the Backdoor to Reality" | "I found the missing step in every manifestation method" |
| State-based (Olly's strongest angle) | "I'm too rich to care about manifestation (copy my state)" | "Copy this heart state and watch your reality shift" |
| Specific proof | "I created a new reality by counting to 50" | "How I created $22,000 in 60 days using my heart" |
| Contrarian stop/start | "I don't manifest. I know — and reality adjusts." | "I don't try to manifest. I do this instead." |
| Lost/hidden method | "How I Finally Manifested Fast (Neville Goddard's 3 Truths)" | "The lost step every manifestation teacher skips" |
| Parenthetical second hook | "One manifestation exercise to RULE them all." | "The missing piece in every manifestation method (use your heart)" |
| Superlative + specific detail | "Most Powerful Manifestation Meditation You'll Ever Try. 22 min." | "The most powerful heart exercise you'll ever try (22 minutes)" |

**Key structural notes:**
- First-person "I" titles consistently outperform third-person instruction — use for personal story and proof content
- "Copy my state" / "copy this" framing is Olly's specific differentiator — nobody else in the niche owns the state angle
- Specificity creates credibility: "counting to 50", "22 min", "$22,000 in 60 days"

### Type of Post

Determined by the **primary job of the video**, not the content inside it:
- **Belief-Changing** — opens with a belief Rachel holds, builds the argument and science, lands the reframe. Personal stories and client proof can appear inside as proof layers
- **Practical** — the primary job is to take Rachel through an actual practice she does during the video
- **Client Success** — the primary job is to prove the mechanism works through a real client's result

---

### Hook

- Must match the title — same emotional register, same promise
- **Lock the title first. Then write the hook.** Never draft both at the same time
- Never assume knowledge Rachel doesn't have — she knows the techniques, not necessarily the science behind them
- Lead with what she has done or is experiencing, not what she knows intellectually
- **For Belief-Changing videos:** lead with the belief she holds, in her own words — then pivot to the reframe
- **For Practical videos:** lead with the frustration this practice addresses — keep the case for it short, she came to do something
- **For Client Success videos:** lead with the specific result — make it concrete before anything else
- Hook structure: (1) what Rachel has been doing or experiencing → (2) the surprising reframe → (3) curiosity gap that keeps her watching
- Plain, direct voice — no hype, no influencer energy
- **When the title uses Neville Goddard language** (wish fulfilled, feel the end, SATS), the hook must use his specific vocabulary throughout

---

### Outcome

A strong Outcome answers:
1. What does Rachel now **believe** that she didn't before?
2. What does she now understand about **why nothing has worked**?
3. What is the **programme** she's been running — and where did it come from?
4. How does this connect directly back to the **title**?

The Outcome must be specific to the angle of this video — not a generic benefit statement.

**For Practical videos — Outcome is experience-led:**
Add a third layer: what did she *do and feel* during the walkthrough, and what does that experience mean? Structure: (1) what she noticed in her body, (2) what that means (resistance = old programme, not failure), (3) what she now wants to do next.

**Never write:** "a framework for why change has to go deeper" — vague, unprovable. Name the specific belief shift and the specific programme.

The programme sources (ref `memory/model-for-health.md`):
- 0–7 years: theta state, no filter, beliefs downloaded as truth
- 7–14 years: identity and social beliefs reinforced
- Trauma at any age: installed instantly and buried deep
- Chronic stress, relationships, illness, media, culture

### Description

Fixed format — CTAs always first, then description copy:

```
👉 Download the free Heart Activation Meditation here: [meditation UTM link]

⏩ Find out more about the Heart Creator Program here: [coaching application UTM link]

[3-paragraph description — punchy, not padded]
Paragraph 1: Name the problem Rachel recognises
Paragraph 2: What this video breaks down
Paragraph 3: The reframe or the stakes — what's actually going on
```

UTM structure:
`utm_source=youtube&utm_medium=description&utm_campaign=meditation&utm_content=[video-slug]`
`utm_source=youtube&utm_medium=description&utm_campaign=coaching&utm_content=[video-slug]`

**UTM slug must always match the current title.** When the title changes, update the slug in description and pinned comment at the same time.

**Voice:** plain, felt language. Not clinical or mechanistic. First person — Olly's voice speaking directly, never third person.

**For Practical videos:** para 3 must flag that a live practice is in the video. "Not a concept. Something you actually do."

**CTA copy — name the problem and outcome, not the mechanism.** "Activate your heart and create your reality" beats "experience Heart Coherence."

### Thumbnail

Always populate the Thumbnail idea field. Read `memory/youtube-thumbnail-shot-list.md` first and name the specific shot (number + name) that fits the video's emotional register. Never suggest a thumbnail without grounding it in a real shot from the list.

**Thumbnail idea field must include all five:**
1. Shot number and name (e.g. "Shot 2 — The Revelation")
2. Layout — which portion of the frame each element occupies
3. Text overlay — exact wording, size hierarchy, and which word carries the accent colour
4. Reading direction — how the eye travels across the image
5. What each image element represents for Rachel — who the figure is, what the scene means, why it connects to the argument

**Design rules:**
- **Layout:** Face right (half the frame), overlay image top-left with soft circular/feathered crop, text bottom-centre or bottom-left
- **Font:** Mont Bold, Star White `#f8f4ff`. Magenta `#d946ef` for accent — underline or colour one key word
- **Text:** 2–5 words. All caps for punchy hooks. Title case + ellipsis for curiosity-gap hooks
- **Background:** Space Black `#080010` default

## 4. Content Register

**Aspirational is the default — not pain-led.**

Top-performing content in this niche (Dispenza, Abraham Hicks, Neville Goddard creators) is outcome-led and empowering. Pain-led content has a place for cold audiences and belief-changing hooks — but should not dominate the plan.

| Register | When to use |
|---|---|
| Aspirational (default) | Titles, hooks and thumbnails across all three types |
| Pain-led | Cold audience hooks, validation moments, belief-naming in Belief-Changing videos |

**Aspirational title patterns for Olly:**
- "How to feel your future as real — and make it stay"
- "Create your reality from the heart — here's how"
- "How to finally feel like the person you're trying to become"
- "The science behind creating your reality from elevated emotion"
- "Why your heart is the most powerful tool you have for creation"

## 5. Rules and Constraints

### Never
- Never write description copy in third person — always Olly's voice in first person
- Never suggest a thumbnail idea not grounded in the actual shot list — cite a specific shot number and name
- Never put a statistic or science fact in the title — statistics go inside the video as proof
- Never write a title over 9 words (parentheticals excluded)
- Never write a title that could belong to a generic self-help video — must signal reality creation so Rachel identifies it as for her
- Never assume Rachel knows the science behind techniques she uses — she knows what to do, not why it works
- Never assume Rachel can visualise — she may not be able to at all. Lead with effort and willpower as the universal experience
- Never open a Practical hook with the technique name — open with the frustration it addresses
- Never write a Practical Outcome that only covers understanding — name what Rachel did and felt during the walkthrough
- Never write a Practical description without flagging the live practice in para 3
- Never use generic meditation language in a hook when the title targets Neville Goddard practitioners — use his specific vocabulary (SATS, wish fulfilled, assume the feeling of the end, feel the end)
- Never default to a pain-led title without first testing an aspirational version of the same idea
- Never let the content plan be predominantly pain-led — aspirational is the default in this niche
- Never use em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators in any copy
- Never use a comma before 'and' or 'or'

### Process
- Lock the title before writing the hook — the hook follows the approved title, never a draft
- Draft all fields in conversation first, present to Olly for review, wait for explicit confirmation before pushing to Notion

### Transcript-to-Description Workflow
Once a video is filmed, use this workflow to generate the description before publishing:
1. Olly uploads the video as **Unlisted** in YouTube Studio
2. YouTube auto-generates a transcript (usually within minutes to an hour)
3. Olly copies the transcript from YouTube Studio (Subtitles tab → click the video → copy auto-transcript) and pastes it into the conversation
4. Generate the description and pinned comment from the transcript
5. Push description and pinned comment to the Notion entry
6. Olly copies from Notion into YouTube Studio and sets the video to **Public**

**Why unlisted first:** YouTube's algorithmic evaluation begins at the point of public visibility, not upload time. Uploading as Unlisted has zero impact on reach or performance when the video goes Public. This is confirmed by YouTube's own documentation and widely validated by YouTube growth experts.
