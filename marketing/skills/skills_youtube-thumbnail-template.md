# YouTube Thumbnail Template Guide — Olly Henson Coaching

> 6 template variations. Build them once in Canva, save to YouTube Thumbnails → Template folder.
> Every new thumbnail = duplicate → swap photo → change text → export. Under 10 minutes.

---

## Before You Start — Canva Setup Checklist

Make sure these are ready in your Canva Brand Kit (Olly Henson Coaching):
- ✅ Colours saved: Space Black `#080010`, Deep Purple `#1a0535`, Magenta `#d946ef`, Star White `#f8f4ff`
- ✅ Font saved: Mont Bold
- ✅ Stars Background PNG uploaded to Assets folder
- ✅ A selection of your expression photos uploaded to Assets folder

All 6 templates use the same canvas size: **1280 x 720px** (YouTube Thumbnail)

---

## Template 1 — Face Right, Text Left (THE WORKHORSE)

**When to use:** Default. Works for almost every video.

**Layer structure (bottom to top):**
1. Background rectangle — full canvas, fill `#080010` (Space Black)
2. Your photo — right half of canvas (640–1280px wide), full height, no crop circle, no border
3. Dark gradient overlay — left to right, `#080010` to transparent, covers left 60% (softens the photo edge)
4. Hook text — Mont Bold, 110–130px, Star White `#f8f4ff`, positioned top-left quadrant
5. (Optional) single accent word — same font, Magenta `#d946ef`

**Text zone:** x: 60px, y: 80px, width: 600px
**Photo zone:** x: 620px, y: 0px, width: 660px, height: 720px

**Example in use:** "Feel it first" — text left, your face right, expression: quiet certainty

## Template 2 — Face Left, Text Right

**When to use:** Variation so thumbnails don't all feel identical. Good when your expression naturally looks left.

**Layer structure (bottom to top):**
1. Background rectangle — full canvas, fill `#080010`
2. Your photo — left half of canvas (0–660px), full height
3. Dark gradient overlay — right to left, `#080010` to transparent, covers right 60%
4. Hook text — Mont Bold, 110–130px, Star White, positioned top-right quadrant
5. (Optional) accent word in Magenta

**Text zone:** x: 660px, y: 80px, width: 560px
**Photo zone:** x: 0px, y: 0px, width: 660px, height: 720px

**Example in use:** "Still in your head?" — your face left, text right, expression: soft curiosity

---

## Template 3 — Stars Background (COSMIC / CONCEPT)

**When to use:** Big concept videos, mission content, anything where the vibe should feel expansive or cinematic. Less grounded, more elevated.

**Layer structure (bottom to top):**
1. Stars Background PNG — full canvas, stretched to fit
2. Dark overlay — full canvas rectangle, `#080010` at 50% opacity (dims the stars so face pops)
3. Your photo — right two-thirds of canvas, full height, slight purple colour overlay at 20% opacity
4. Hook text — Mont Bold, 120px, Star White, left side
5. Accent word or line — Magenta `#d946ef`

**Tip:** For this template, a slightly more upward-looking or expansive expression works best.

**Example in use:** "Your heart knows" — stars behind you, looking slightly upward, text left

---

## Template 4 — With Prop / Overlay (THREE-ZONE)

**When to use:** When you have a physical prop (notebook, chart, book) or want to show a visual concept alongside your face. Great for educational content.

**Layout:** Face takes up the right third, prop/image takes up the middle third, text on the far left.

**Layer structure (bottom to top):**
1. Background rectangle — full canvas, `#080010`
2. Your photo — right third (860–1280px), full height
3. Prop image or AI-generated visual — centre (380–860px), slight border or glow effect
4. Left gradient — `#080010` to transparent, covers left 40%
5. Hook text — Mont Bold, 100–110px, Star White, far left
6. Optional: small label under prop in Muted Lavender `#c4b5fd`, Inter font, 28px

**Example in use:** "The missing piece" — text left, diagram centre, your face right with a "wait for it" expression

---

## Template 5 — Face Centre, Text Top + Bottom

**When to use:** Strong single-expression shots where the face is the whole story. Works when the hook naturally splits into two short lines.

**Layout:** Face dominates the centre-right, text sits above and below, not competing with the face.

**Layer structure (bottom to top):**
1. Background rectangle — full canvas, `#1a0535` (Deep Purple — slightly warmer than pure black)
2. Your photo — centre-right, full height, takes up 70% of canvas width
3. Left edge gradient — `#1a0535` to transparent
4. Top text — Mont Bold, 80px, Star White, top-left, above the face
5. Bottom text — Mont Bold, 80px, Magenta `#d946ef`, bottom-left, below the face

**Example in use:** Top: "NOT" / Bottom: "A MIND GAME" — face centre, two-line split, expression: calm conviction

---

## Template 6 — Minimal Dark (CLEAN / STATEMENT)

**When to use:** When the hook is so strong it needs no visual noise. One face, one line, nothing else.

**Layout:** Face takes up roughly half the canvas. Text is large, single line, almost filling the remaining half.

**Layer structure (bottom to top):**
1. Background rectangle — full canvas, `#080010`
2. Subtle vignette — dark radial gradient from edges inward (adds depth without adding clutter)
3. Your photo — either side, full height, no effects
4. Single hook line — Mont Bold, 130–150px, Star White, opposite side to face
5. No accent colour, no subtitle — the contrast of white on black is the design

**Example in use:** "Too easy" — face left, giant white text right, deadpan expression

## How to Save and Use Each Template

**Saving:**
1. Complete the template design
2. Right-click the design title → Rename it clearly (e.g. "TEMPLATE — Face Right Text Left")
3. Move to YouTube Thumbnails → Template folder in Canva

**Using:**
1. Open the template
2. Click the three dots → **Make a copy**
3. Rename the copy with the video title
4. Swap the photo: click the image placeholder → drag your shot from Assets folder
5. Click the text → type your hook
6. Export → JPG → move to Published folder

**Never edit the original template — always make a copy first.**
