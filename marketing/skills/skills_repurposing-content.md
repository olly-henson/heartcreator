## 1. Purpose

**Trigger phrase: "Repurpose content"**

Take one content idea and produce three platform-native pieces — a YouTube Community Post, an Instagram Story, and a Broadcast Email — then push all three into the correct Notion databases ready to post.

The content is not copied across. It is adapted. Same core idea. Different format, length, and platform language for each.

**Workflow:** Create and schedule YT posts first. Push email and stories only when Olly asks. Never proactively.

## 2. Idea Generation

Follow the same process used for Instagram Reels and YouTube Long-Form videos.

### Step 1 — Load the foundation files

Before generating any idea, read:
- `memory/argument_sheet.md` — the full argument framework; the idea must connect to at least one stage
- `memory/prospect_intel.md` — real prospect language; use this to find the right angle and phrasing
- `memory/review_content.md` — cross-book Rachel summary; real customer frustrations, beliefs, language
- `memory/avatar-rachel.md` — primary audience avatar
- `memory/meditation-nurture-sequence-LIVE.md` — the live 10-email nurture sequence; make sure broadcast emails are not duplicating content Rachel has already received

### Step 1b — If Olly provides existing content as the source

When Olly shares a Reel script, video transcript, or any existing piece of content as the starting point, **skip idea generation from scratch.** Instead:

1. Extract the core belief/pain point and argument stage from the provided content
2. Confirm the post type
3. Proceed directly to platform adaptation

The source content's structure, length, and delivery style are irrelevant. Adapt the core insight only — do not replicate the Reel format, speaking style, or CTA in the YT Community post.

### Step 2 — Generate the idea

A strong repurposable idea has:
- A clear hook angle (a belief Rachel holds, a frustration she lives with, a moment from Olly's story, a question she's never seen answered)
- A core argument or shift (what changes by the end — what does she see differently?)
- A natural fit across all three platforms (if it only works on one, it's not a repurposing idea — generate a different one)

Use the same content pillars as Reels and long-form:
- Argument-based — why she's stuck, what the actual cause is, why Heart Coherence is different
- Educational — nervous system, Heart Coherence, the heart itself, the subconscious, brainwaves
- Personal story — chronic illness thread, conscious creation / $13k month thread
- Mission — why Olly does this work
- Belief-Changing — name a specific belief she holds, validate it, reframe it logically
- Case Study / Client Proof — Isabell, Anas (only real results)

### Step 3 — Name the idea

Before writing any platform copy, state:
- **The core idea:** one sentence
- **The belief or pain point it addresses:** what Rachel is experiencing right now
- **The argument stage it connects to:** Pain / Actual Cause / New Mechanism / New Belief / Promised Land
- **The post type:** Personal Story / Belief-Changing / Personal Mission / Case Study / Engagement

Confirm the idea is strong before proceeding to platform adaptation.

#### The Title Field — Above-the-Fold Hook

The Title field is the first 1–2 lines of the post — what YouTube shows before "Read more." Must create enough curiosity that someone clicks to read the rest.

**Three proven approaches:**

**1. Two-line build with ellipsis**
```
Another week...

And nothing's changed.
```

**2. Prospect quote in quotation marks**
```
"I know healing is possible, I just can't make it work for me."

That's something I hear a lot.
```

**3. Unfinished open question**
```
Have you ever noticed that every time you try something new to feel better...
```

Rules: max 2 lines above the fold. End on an ellipsis, gut-punch statement, or unfinished thought. Must speak to Rachel's lived experience — not a concept. Name something specific to her: the years of doing the work, the specific moment she experiences (e.g. in meditation feeling like her future self), the crash-back. Generic statements like "you can't feel it" are too vague — name what she actually experiences so she recognises herself immediately.

**Content register:** YT Community Posts default to pain-led (meeting Rachel in her frustration to earn the click). But the aspirational register is also valid and should be used for variety — especially for Mission posts, Belief-Changing posts, and HCP CTA posts. An aspirational above-the-fold line leads with the identity or outcome she is moving toward rather than the frustration she is stuck in. See `skills_youtube-longform.md` Content Register Balance section for full guidance and examples. The fold technique still applies regardless of register.

**The "But....." fold technique:** End the above-the-fold section with "But....." as a standalone word at the end of the second line. YouTube cuts to "Read more" immediately after — leaving "But....." hanging right at the fold forces the click. The question or reframe then opens the body below the fold. This is more powerful than ending on a complete thought.

**Character limit:** The Title field must be approximately 150–155 characters (around 27 words) to hit the fold correctly. Confirmed from a live post: 153 characters / 27 words cut perfectly with "But....." landing right before "Read more."

Always count the Title field before pushing to Notion. If it's significantly over 155 characters, tighten the wording. If it's under 140, it may not reach the fold at all.

Approved example: "I know that you've probably been doing the work for years. And sometimes, in meditation, you do actually feel like the person you're trying to become. But....." — then "Why can't you maintain this feeling?" opens the body.

#### Format Rules

- **Line length:** Short. One or two sentences per line maximum.
- **White space:** Every single line separated by a blank line. Critical for copy-paste formatting.
- **Ellipsis:** Use for pacing and dramatic rhythm throughout the body. "Back in 2018..." / "But that didn't stop me..."
- **Lists:** ➡️ for symptoms/neutral lists. ✅ for positive outcomes/Promised Land.
- **No em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators.** Use a full stop or comma.
- **No comma before 'and' or 'or'.**
- **British English** throughout.

#### Sign-off options

- **Default:** `Olly 😎` — confirmed as the standard close for YT Community Posts 2026-06-23
- Casual (when gifting something): `Enjoy 😎`
- Warm (ends with a gift): `Enjoy, Olly`
- Soft (occasional variation): `To your health, happiness and success. Olly`
- Reflective (fits the story): e.g. `I hope you do too. Olly`
- Engagement post: end with the question — no sign-off

#### CTAs

**Standard CTA — Meditation (primary):**
Name Rachel's current state specifically — not a generic invitation.
```
I've dropped a free Heart Activation Meditation in the comments below for you.

This will be really helpful if you're currently struggling to see results and really feel like your future is already happening.
```
Adapt the second sentence to fit the specific post. Do not use generic "If you'd like to experience Heart Coherence."

**Direct CTA — Heart Creator Program:**
```
If you're ready to stop understanding this and start actually feeling it — the link to apply for The Heart Creator Program is in the comments below.
```

Always adapt the HCP CTA wording to the specific post's argument. Do not use the generic line verbatim if the post has a distinct angle.

Good example (post about analytical minds stuck in beta state): "If you're ready to make the shift from thinking about your future to actually feeling it. The link to apply for The Heart Creator Program is in the comments below."

Poor example (generic, disconnected from post): "If you're ready to stop understanding this and start actually feeling it — the link to apply for The Heart Creator Program is in the comments below."

> **CTA frequency rule:** Only one CTA per week on YouTube Community Posts — the first post published on a Monday. All other posts that week end without a CTA or Pinned Comment. Do not add a CTA by default. Only include one if Olly confirms it is the Monday post.
> 
> **Monday CTA destination:** The Monday CTA links to the Heart Creator Program only (`https://ollyhenson.com/coaching-application`). Never the meditation on YouTube Community Posts.
>
> **CTA override:** Olly may assign a CTA to any post regardless of the day or frequency rule. When he does, apply it without question and adapt the wording to fit that post's specific argument.

#### Belief-Changing Structure

```
HOOK — THE BELIEF
State the belief directly in Rachel's own words. Use quotation marks.
The quote must name a specific technique — visualise, meditate, affirm — not just "do the work" generically.
Good: "I just need to visualise better and be more consistent."
Bad: "I just need to be more consistent."

IDENTIFIER
"I hear this a lot." Then name the context in 1–2 lines so Rachel feels seen.

VALIDATE
Meet her inside it. "If you've spent years trying things that haven't lasted, of course you'd feel that way."

REFRAME — use questions, not declarations
Use "But what if..." questions to introduce the reframe. Questions feel like an invitation to think.
Good: "But what if it's nothing to do with the technique? What if it's the STATE you're doing it in?"
Never state the reframe as a flat declaration — Rachel resists being told. She arrives at it herself through the question.

SURVIVAL MODE — soften it
When naming survival mode as the cause, soften with "just" and "mainly":
"It's just because you're still mainly operating from a survival-based state."
Never make Rachel feel clinically assessed or blamed.

HEART LANGUAGE — not Heart Coherence
In Belief-Changing posts, use "opening your heart" / "getting into your heart" language.
Do NOT name "Heart Coherence" in Belief-Changing posts — that lives in Educational posts.
Keep it warm and felt, not technical.

CLOSE — plant the seed, no CTA
Close with a forward-looking line that plants the seed without pushing:
"Getting out of your head and into your heart is where this starts."
"Once you open your heart, this all changes."
Rachel builds the belief herself across multiple posts. The CTA comes later.
```

Key reframe patterns:
- **The learning loop:** "So you end up having to go and learn more, to still not be able to really feel it. This pattern can go on for years."
- **One-sentence logic:** The reframe is a single clean sentence — not a science paragraph.
- **Promised land — specific:** "Your imagination feels like a creative tool rather than something you have to force." Not: "You'll finally be able to create the life you want."

#### Personal Story Structure

```
HOOK — specific fact, number, date, or real situation. Never vague.
Approved: "In November and December 2018... My income grew by 364%."
Rejected: "Back in 2018... Something shifted."

SETUP — specific life circumstances. Housing situation, financial reality, recent events.
"I was back living with my parents at 28. I'd just returned from travelling Europe. I'd taken a serious head injury in September that was affecting my ability to work."

SYMPTOMS / WHAT IT LOOKED LIKE — ➡️ list, 5–8 items. Lead with what Rachel recognises.

TURNING POINT — "What I realised was..." / "Things only changed when..."

THE MECHANISM — introduce Heart Coherence naturally, not as a pitch. Always "something called Heart Coherence."

THE RESULT — specific and grounded. Not vague wellness promises.

CLOSE — CTA, soft sign-off, or engagement question.
```

#### Format Rules for Stories

Instagram Stories are a series of vertical slides. Each slide is short — 1–3 lines of text. The reader taps through, so the content in "What To Talk About" is a brief direction for what to cover across the slides, not a fully scripted post.

- Keep "What To Talk About" to 2–4 sentences max. It's a content brief, not copy.
- The hook is pulled directly from the linked YT Community Post or email — same wording, not rewritten.
- British English throughout.
- No em-dashes or hyphens as clause separators.

#### Format Rules for Broadcast Email

- **Short lines.** Single sentences as their own paragraph.
- **Ellipsis (...)** used deliberately for forward momentum and open loops.
- **Plain language.** No clinical terms, no wellness jargon.
- **British English** throughout.
- **First person, conversational.** Sounds like a message from someone who has been through it.
- **Never preachy.** Never "you deserve this."
- **Length:** 150–300 words. Up to 400 for personal story or framework emails.
- **`{{contact.first_name}}`** for personalised first name.

#### Hook Adaptation from YT Community Post

In a YouTube Community Post, "But....." is placed at the end of the Title field to sit right at the fold and force the "Read more" click.

In email, there is no fold — the full body loads. So "But..." moves to its own line after the opening sentences, where it creates a pause and builds tension before the question. The structural job is different even though the word is the same.

Always adapt the hook position when moving from YT Community Post to email — do not copy the Title field verbatim.

#### Strip Platform Language

When adapting from a YT Community Post or Story, always:
- Remove any line containing "comments below" — this is YouTube language. It reads as a mistake in an email.
- Remove "DM me" or ManyChat references — not relevant in email.
- Do not copy the community post CTA verbatim. Replace with the email CTA format.

#### CTA Format — Non-Negotiable

CTAs in broadcast email are always inline hyperlinked text — never a plain URL, never `===> HERE`.

**HCP CTA — approved wording (confirmed 2026-06-23):**
```
If you're ready to shift the state you're creating from, you can find out more about The Heart Creator Program and apply here.
```
Where **here** is hyperlinked to:
```
https://ollyhenson.com/coaching-application?utm_source=email&utm_medium=broadcast&utm_campaign=hcp&utm_content=[subject-line-slug]
```

**HCP CTA build-up pattern (confirmed 2026-06-23):**
Use three conditional lines before naming the programme — this is the strongest pattern:
```
If you'd like creating your reality to feel really good...

If you want synchronicities to start appearing naturally...

And you want to always feel confident that what you're wanting to create is actually on the way...

My 1-2-1 Heart Creator Program will help you do just that.

You can find out more about it here
```

Always include `utm_content` with the subject line in lowercase, hyphens replacing spaces. Example: `utm_content=positive-thinking-does-not-create-your-reality`

Also add the bare URL on the next line in the Notion CTA field so Olly can verify the link before loading into GHL.

**Rejected format:**
```
I've built a Heart Activation Meditation for you to start using ===> HERE
```
Why rejected: `===> HERE` format is over the top. Olly confirmed 2026-06-23 — always hyperlink the natural word in the sentence.

## 5. What Never to Write

- Never fabricate client results — only use real proof from Isabell and Anas
- Never use banned words: game changer, deep dive, synergy, leverage, hustle, grind, unlock, skyrocket, supercharge
- Never assume Rachel knows what Heart Coherence is — always "something called Heart Coherence" on first mention
- Never use em-dashes as clause separators — in post copy, email body, outcome fields, image idea, anywhere. Replace with a full stop or restructure the sentence.
- Never use a comma before 'and' or 'or'
- Never open a Personal Story with a vague experiential statement — open with a specific fact, number, or date
- Never assume Rachel can visualise — lead with effort and willpower as the universal experience
- Never copy a YouTube/community UTM into an email
- Never leave "comments below" language in an email version
- Never sound like a wellness influencer or press release
- Never run the same email type twice in a row
- Never name the cause before the symptoms have landed — go straight from the hook question into the specific signs Rachel recognises in herself. The explanation of why follows only after she's nodded along to the list
- Never add a bridging sentence that pre-explains what the symptom list is about to prove — cut it. If a sentence names the cause before the symptoms, it robs the symptom list of its landing power. Example of what to cut: "These doubts typically occur when our nervous system has become too familiar with operating from a state of survival." — this explained the mechanism before Rachel had recognised herself in the symptoms.
- Never use vague emotional language — always name specific emotions. Write "joy, freedom and gratitude" not "elevated emotions." Write "survival mode" in one clean sentence, not a paragraph of explanation.
- Never write "nothing is changing" when Rachel's experience is "nothing is happening" — her language is passive. Things happen *to* her or don't. Use her framing.
- Never write a bridging or transition line as a multi-sentence paragraph — it lands harder as a single short standalone sentence. "Our nervous system has to be balanced." One line. Done.
- Never introduce Heart Coherence or the solution before the problem has fully landed — one post, one job
- Never over-explain the pinned comment — the post body carries the context
- Never write a hook, subject line or Story hook using a vague action word without naming the specific subject. "Consistency", "showing up", "trying harder", "doing the work" mean nothing without context. Always name what specifically — manifesting, meditations, reality creation, affirmations. Every hook must make complete sense to someone who has never seen any other piece of this content. Test: cover everything except the hook. Does it still tell you exactly what the content is about? If not, rewrite it.
- Never adapt an Engagement post to email or Instagram Story — the format doesn't translate. Skip it entirely.
- Never name "Heart Coherence" in a Belief-Changing post — use "opening your heart" / "getting into your heart" language. Heart Coherence belongs in Educational posts.
- Never use `===> HERE` in any email CTA — hyperlink the natural word in the sentence ("here", "apply here"). Confirmed rejected 2026-06-23.
- Never push email campaigns or Instagram Stories proactively — only when Olly asks, after YT posts are scheduled.
