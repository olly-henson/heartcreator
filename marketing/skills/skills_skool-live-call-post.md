# Skool Live Call Post — CEO Olly Henson

> Reference template for writing a post inside the free Skool Community that invites members to a live training. Use this whenever Olly asks for a Skool post ahead of a live call.

---

## When to use this skill

Use this when Olly says any of:
* "Write me a Skool post for the live call"
* "Invite the community to tonight's training"
* "Promote tonight's live training in Skool"
* Anything about a live training, live call, or live session happening inside the Skool community

---

## What you need to gather first

Before writing, confirm with Olly:

1. **Date** (e.g. Wed 13 May)
2. **Time in London** (e.g. 8 to 9 PM)
3. **Name of the technique or topic** being taught (e.g. Heart Coherence)
4. **One-line description** of what the technique does in nervous-system terms
5. **The felt outcomes** people walk away with (3-4 benefits)
6. **Any specific time zones** to highlight (default list is below)

If anything is missing, ask for it before drafting.

---

## Structure (in order)

1. **Title** — `Live tonight. [Technique]: a practice for [outcome 1], [outcome 2] and [outcome 3]`
2. **One-line opener** — `Tonight inside the community...`
3. **The hook** — `I'm running a live training.`
4. **Set up the technique** — `On this training, you'll be learning and practising a heart-based technique called:` then the name on its own line with the 💚 emoji
5. **Why it works** — 1-2 short paragraphs in plain language about how the technique shifts the nervous system out of survival mode
6. **The felt-outcomes block** — `What it tends to leave people with:` followed by 4 benefits, each on its own line, each starting with ✅
7. **The personal credibility line** — `[Technique] is one of the practices I wish someone had handed me years ago, before I tried everything else first.`
8. **The time zone block** — `Here's the time of the call across the most common time zones so that you don't miss it:` followed by each zone on its own line starting with ➡️
9. **The live encouragement** — `If you can be there live, please do.` then `There's something different about practising this live together.`
10. **The replay note** — `If you can't make it and someone is on the call, the replay will go up afterwards in the Classroom.`
11. **The soft CTA** — `Comment below or react to this post if you're planning to come.` then `It helps me know who I'm preparing for.`
12. **Sign-off** — `See you tonight. 😎` then `Olly`

## Formatting rules (Skool-specific)

* **Short paragraphs.** Almost every sentence sits on its own line. Use a blank line between every line for breathing room. Skool reads like a chat feed, not a blog.
* **Emoji is allowed and expected on this post type** — this overrides the general "minimal emoji" rule in the brand voice file. The conventions are:
  * 💚 next to the name of the technique
  * ✅ for each item in the "What it tends to leave people with" block
  * ➡️ for each time zone line
  * 😎 in the sign-off
* **No em dashes.** Use full stops or commas. Never use `—`.
* **Time format.** Write "8 to 9 PM," not "8-9 PM." Avoid the hyphen.
* **British English** throughout. "Practising," "realised," "personalised."
* **Banned words** still apply — no "game changer," "deep dive," "unlock," "supercharge," etc. (See `skills_brand-voice.md`.)

## Default time zone list

Calculate fresh each time based on the London time and the date (account for DST). The default zones to include:

* London (BST or GMT depending on date)
* Central Europe (CEST or CET)
* New York / Toronto (EDT or EST)
* Chicago (CDT or CST)
* Denver (MDT or MST)
* Los Angeles / Vancouver (PDT or PST)
* Sydney / Melbourne (AEST or AEDT)
* Auckland (NZST or NZDT)

Note Australia and New Zealand will usually be the morning of the *next* day. Always check.

## Voice notes

* Warm but matter-of-fact. Olly is the calm coach, not a hype merchant.
* Acknowledge what people have already tried (doctors, alternative practitioners) only if Olly asks for that angle. The default version above skips it because the Skool audience already knows the story.
* The argument-based pillar is always running underneath: the body needs to feel safe before anything else works. Reference this naturally if there's space, but don't over-explain.
* Soft CTA. Never "register now" or "sign up." It's "comment below or react."
