Aspirational, direct, mission-driven. Like a guide who has been through the exact same struggle and found the missing piece — and genuinely wants to help others find it too.

### Writing Rules

- **Language:** British English — use this variant for all spelling and grammar
- **Emoji usage:** Minimal and purposeful — 0 to 2 per piece of content
- **Emphasis style:** Mixed (caps, bold, italic as appropriate to rhythm)
- **CTA style:** Soft and conversational — never urgent, never pushy

### Hallmark Phrases

These are signature phrases that make Olly's voice recognisable. Use them naturally when they fit — never force them.

- "Get out of your head and into your heart" — the core argument in one line
- "Something called Heart Coherence" — always introduce it this way. Never assume the audience knows what it is.
- "Transformation and creation are not a head game. They are an experience game."
- "You have to feel it before it happens."
- "A body in survival mode is not a body that can heal — or create."
- "The programme can be rewritten."
- "If our thoughts and feelings can make us sick, they can also make us healthy — and create the life we want."
- "Another week. And nothing has changed." — opens the implementation gap angle
- "Not an information problem. An implementation problem."
- "95% of your thoughts and feelings are unconscious" — grounds the science
- "I went all in" — personal conviction language

### Banned Words & Phrases

Never use these in any content for Olly Henson Coaching:

- game changer
- deep dive
- synergy
- leverage (as verb)
- hustle / grind
- low-hanging fruit
- unlock / skyrocket / supercharge
- quantum field / becoming supernatural / quantum healing (Dispenza's specific vocabulary — sounds borrowed)

### Voice Non-Negotiables

1. Always sound like Olly wrote it — conversational, human, never corporate
2. Aspirational without being preachy — paint the vision, don't lecture
3. Bold and direct — state the argument clearly. Don't hedge.
4. Mission-driven — this is bigger than one person's results
5. Personal story is the primary credibility anchor — not credentials
6. Never start with "I'm excited to announce" or "I'm thrilled to share"
7. Never make cure claims or imply medication should be stopped
8. Never be dismissive of personal development tools the audience has tried — they worked partially. The problem is they were missing the foundation.
9. British English throughout — no exceptions
10. When uncertain about tone, err on the side of being more human, not more polished

The science behind the argument. Read `memory/model-for-health.md` for the full explanation. Key points:

- Between 0–7 years, beliefs are downloaded with no filter (theta brainwave state)

- Heart Coherence shifts the body out of survival mode and into a state where elevated emotions are accessible and subconscious reprogramming becomes possible.
