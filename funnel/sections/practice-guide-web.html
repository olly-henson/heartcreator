How to Perform the
Heart Activation Meditation

A simple, powerful practice to move out of your head, into your heart and into the place where real change begins.

The Science Behind It

When you slow your breathing and place your attention on your heart, your heart rhythm shifts into what scientists call heart coherence. A smooth, ordered pattern that directly calms the stress centres of the brain. Your brainwaves slow down, the mental noise clears and you return to feeling more like yourself. This coherent state is where you can affirm, visualise, feel and create from. Not from stress or survival, but from your natural creative power.

The Practice — Step by Step
1
Close Your Eyes and Relax
Close your eyes and let your body soften. Let your shoulders relax. Let your face relax. Let your mind relax. There's nothing you need to do right now.
2
Breathe In Through Your Nose and Out Through Your Mouth
Begin to breathe in through your nose and out through your mouth. Let your breath find its natural rhythm.
3
Slow Your Breath Down
Breathe the air slowly in. And as you breathe out, breathe the air slowly out. Slowly in and slowly out. There's no rush.
4
Bring Your Attention to Your Heart
Guide your attention to the area of your heart. The centre of your chest. Let any tightness in that area relax.
5
Breathe In and Out of Your Heart
As you continue to breathe slowly in and slowly out, imagine that the air you're breathing is flowing in and out of your heart. Slowly in and slowly out.
6
Feel the Lightness and the Calm
Feel the lightness and the calm as you breathe slowly in and slowly out of your heart. When you're ready, slowly bring your awareness back to your body and open your eyes.

Remember: You're not trying to force anything. Relax, breathe and let your heart do the work. The lightness and calm you feel is heart coherence activating. That's your creative state. Stay there as long as feels good.
