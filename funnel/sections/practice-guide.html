How to Perform the
Heart Activation Meditation

A simple, powerful practice to move out of your head, into your heart and into the place where real change begins.

The Science Behind It

When you slow your breathing and place your attention on your heart, your heart rhythm shifts into what scientists call heart coherence. A smooth, ordered pattern that directly calms the stress centres of the brain. Your brainwaves slow down, the mental noise clears and you return to feeling more like yourself. This coherent state is where you can affirm, visualise, feel, and create from. Not from stress or survival, but from your natural creative power.

The Practice — Step by Step
1
Breathe In. Breathe Out. Let Everything Go.
Take a deep breath in and as you exhale consciously release whatever you've been holding. You don't need to carry it in here.
2
Relax Your Body, One Part at a Time
Allow every muscle to soften — from your face down to your feet. The guided meditation will take you through this. Simply follow along and let go.
3
Place Your Attention on Your Heart
Gently bring your awareness to the area in the centre of your chest. Not a thought about it — just a feeling of presence there. Rest your attention softly in that space.
4
Breathe Through Your Heart
Imagine the air is flowing in and out of your heart directly. In through the heart on the inhale. Out through the heart on the exhale. Keep your focus gently anchored there.
5
Slow It Down — This Is the Key
Breathe slowly in and slowly out. There's no rush. As you breathe slowly through your heart, notice a growing sense of inner ease. This is heart coherence activating. Stay here. This is your creative ground.

Remember: The goal isn't to think your way into this state, it's to feel your way there. Even two minutes of this practice provides six hours of mental, emotional and physical benefits.
