# Plateau Breakthrough — Client Resource

**Use when:** A client reports that Heart Coherence worked for the first few days then stopped working or became harder to access. This is the most common plateau pattern and has a specific explanation.

**Source:** Video transcript (external — Heart Coherence plateau mechanism)

---

## The Core Pattern This Addresses

Client reports:
- Heart Coherence felt amazing on days 1–2
- By day 3–4 it required more effort
- By day 4–5 it felt like it stopped working entirely
- Client is wondering if they are doing it wrong, or if it doesn't work for them

**This is not failure. This is the nervous system reversion pattern.**

---

## The Explanation (in plain language for clients)

The nervous system has three states (based on Polyvagal Theory — Dr. Stephen Porges):

- **Shutdown** — freeze, collapse, numbness, depression
- **Survival** — fight or flight, stress, hypervigilance (where most clients live)
- **Safety** — coherence, connection, flow, calm

Most clients have been in Survival mode for months or years. Their nervous system has built superhighways for stress — fast, automatic, deeply reinforced pathways. The pathways for Safety (coherence) are dirt roads.

When a client first practices Heart Coherence, they access the Safety state and it feels extraordinary. But the subconscious nervous system registers this as *unfamiliar* — and unfamiliar equals unsafe. So it pulls them back to Survival mode, which is familiar, even though familiar means stress.

This is why coherence wears off after 72 hours. Not because the practice failed. Because the nervous system hasn't yet been trained to sustain it.

**The solution is not to practice harder. It is to train the nervous system to recognise coherence as the new baseline — to pave the dirt road into a superhighway through consistent, daily repetition.**

---

## Key Points to Share With the Client

1. What they experienced is universal — this is called the reversion cycle and almost everyone goes through it
2. It means the practice worked — they genuinely accessed coherence — their nervous system just pulled them back to the familiar
3. The practice doesn't stop working. Their consistency is what builds the new baseline
4. Each daily session is laying another layer of road. The highway gets built over time, not in a single session
5. The goal is to make coherence the new familiar — to make gear 3 (safety) feel as automatic as stress currently does

## How to Use This With a Client

- Share the gear 1/2/3 framework verbally in a coaching call
- Use the "superhighway vs dirt road" metaphor — it lands immediately
- Reframe their experience: they didn't fail, their nervous system did exactly what it was designed to do
- Reinforce that consistency is what builds the new baseline — not intensity
- Ask them to commit publicly to one specific challenge (e.g. "my challenge is remembering to practice before I check my phone") — naming it increases follow-through
- If they are in the programme, point them back to the daily progressive practice structure — this is exactly what it is designed to do
